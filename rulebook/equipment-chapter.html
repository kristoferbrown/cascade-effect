Equipment Basics

The following section contains the stats of the various pieces of combat equipment. When choosing gear at character creation keep in mind your Resources skill and your character concept, you can only start with higher damage weapons if you have points in this skill and it makes sense with your character concept. Later in the game, you can acquire weapons from the circumstances the GM places them in.

Keep in mind the social ramifications of the gear you carry. Bystanders will treat someone in combat armor with an assault rifle very differently than an unarmed person or a person with weapons completely concealed. Many weapons are also illegal to carry or illegal to conceal in most settings.

Speed: This column indicates the speed cost of doing a basic attack with this weapon. Drawing any melee weapon has a speed cost of 3.

Damage: For all melee weapons the damage roll is equal to your Strength plus the number of successes listed in this column.

The concealability of a melee weapon is determined by its speed cost. Weapons with speed costs under 3 can be hidden in any pocket, ones with 3 can be hidden in a jacket, ones with 4 can be hidden in long trench coat, and anything higher can not be concealed.

These stats are for quality melee weapons. For improvised, homemade, or low quality weapons the number listed in the damage column is the number of dice to add to the damage roll, not the number of successes to add to it.

Ranged Weapons

The attack roll for a ranged weapon is based on the attribute Perception and 1 of 3 skills. All firearms use the skill Firearms and all other ranged attacks use the skill Ballistics. Also, all ranged attacks can benefit from the Aim action which is always based on the skill Ballistics, regardless of the weapon used.

The following is a list of basic ranged weapons sorted into sections by their type. The type of weapon determines it's concealability. Handguns and most thrown weapons can be concealed in a pocket or waistband, sub-machine guns can be hidden in a jacket, rifles and shotguns can be concealed in a full length trench coat (although the larger ones like light machine guns and assualt shotguns can not be concealed), and all larger ranged weapons can not be hidden.

When chosing a firearm as a starting weapon at character creation keep in mind the weapon's legality and your character's concept and Resources skill.

Ranged Weapon Stats

All following sections contain tables of stats for ranged weapons. Each weapon will have the following stats.

Weapon Traits

Weapon traits represent any positive or negative special characteristics that an attack may have. Some traits represent the intrinsic properties of a weapon itself, some represent the effects of a permanent modification to the weapon, and some represent attachments that can be added or removed from a weapon. The trait description will clarify which form the trait takes and what kind of weapons it can be applied to.

General Weapon Traits

Small

This weapon can be brought into grapples and used with the Point Blank Attack action. If it is a firearm it can also make use of the Point Blank Shot action. The speed cost of drawing this weapon is 2.

Most pistols and small knives have this trait by design. Small weapons can typically be concealed in a large pocket or a under a short jacket.

Stunning

This weapon deals no damage, instead the results of the damage roll determine how long the target is stunned for. Each success rolled in the damage roll stuns the target until the end of another round, starting with the current round. For example, a damage roll of 1 would prevent the target from acting for the rest of the current round, and a roll of 2 would prevent actions until the end of the next round, and so on. Critical hits do not affect the results of a stunning attack and the location roll only matters for determining whether a target in cover is hit.

Melee weapons with this trait do not add Strength to the damage roll and instead have a set damage roll. Unlike other attacks that deal a minimum of 1 point of damage on hit, it is possible for a stunning weapon to get 0 successes on the damage roll and have no effect. Atrifact functions that increase damage do not improve the damage or stunning power of a stunning weapon.

Electrified tasers and stun guns have this trait and have low damage rolls somewhere between 1+0 and 2+1.

Ballistic Weapon Traits

Bow

The speed cost for this weapon factors in both readying another arrow (if an arrow is readily accessible) and then shooting it. If an arrow has already been readied the speed cost to shoot it is only 2.

In the app, the ammo capacity meter is replaced by a number of arrows for bows. This number represents the total number of arrows you are carrying. The add arrows button allows you to acquire more arrows, if doing so is an action in combat deduct the speed cost manually. Editing the weapon will allow you to manually set the current number of arrows.

Explosive

This weapon fires an explosive projectile and targets a space instead of an enemy. The targeted space is considered the full damage zone, all adjacent spaces are considered the normal zone, and all spaces adjacent to the normal damage zone are considered the splash zone. Characters in the full damage zone are automatically hit and dealt maximum damage to all bodily locations.

Every character in the normal zone that does not use a ranged dodge action or fails at a ranged dodge action is automatically hit and dealt the weapon's normal damage roll to a single location. The location roll can score critical levels, but you cannot adjust the location rolled. Characters that do use a ranged dodge action make their dodge rolls against a single roll of the weapon's normal attack roll. If they succeed at a dodge roll they must move to an adjacent space that either has cover or is in the splash zone, if they move to cover they avoid all damage, if they move into the splash damage zone they are dealt the weapon's damage with all automatic successes replaced with dice.

For the splash zone make a single roll of the weapon's attack roll with all automatic successes replaced by dice. Character's with passive defenses higher than the results of this roll or that make a ranged dodge roll that ties the attack roll avoid all damage, however character's using a ranged dodge action must move out of the splash damage zone or into cover. Characters that do not successfully avoid damage are dealt the weapon's damage roll with all automatic successes replaced with dice. Explosive weapons cannot be used with special combat actions such as Aim, Point Blank Shot, or Stealth Attack.

High Explosive

This trait works as per the normal explosive trait, but it creates a full damage zone that includes the targeted space and all adjacent spaces. The normal zone includes two rows of spaces around the full damage zone. The splash zone includes two rows of spaces around the normal zone. Characters in the normal or splash zones are dealt damage to all locations instead of a single location.

Light Explosive

This trait works as per the normal explosive trait, but it does not create a full damage zone. The targeted space is the normal zone and all adjacent spaces are the the splash zone.

Thrown

The speed cost weapon assumes you are already holding it in your hand. If it is on the ground adjacent to you or stored on your person you need to take Draw Weapon action before you can use it. The range for this weapon is equal to your Strength score. Many smaller melee weapons can also be considered a ranged weapon with this trait.

Firearm Traits

Automatic

The basic attack for this weapon is a 3 round burst, the damage and speed cost listed reflect this (and in the app 3 bullets are automatically deducted each shot). This weapon is capable of the Auto Fire and Spray Actions and gets a bonus on the Suppressive Fire action.

This trait can only be built into a weapon by design, it cannot be granted by attachments or modifications.

Double

This weapon has two barrels and can make either 1 or 2 shots as a single action. Firing 2 shots increases the speed cost by 1. Both shots share attack and location rolls but have separate damage rolls that are added together to get the total damage of the attack.

If this weapon also has the Small or Sawn trait and is used in a Point Blank Shot or Point Blank Attack action only one of the barrel's damage roll is maximized by the action.

This trait can only be added to a weapon by design, it cannot be added by modifications or attachments. Typically only shotguns are designed with this trait.

Extended Magazine

This attachment doubles the normal clip size of the weapon (in the app this doubles whatever number you enter for the clip size). The actions Auto Fire, Spray, and Suppressing Fire only deplete half of the weapon's ammo supply instead of all of it.

Some weapons, such as machine guns, have this trait by design, while others, such as assault rifles and pistols can get this trait by attaching a specially designed magazine. Any weapon with this trait is impossible to conceal on your body.

Laser Sight

A laser sight attachment makes a target easier to hit without aiming. When targeting something within range add one automatic success to basic attack rolls made with this weapon, but not to any special attack actions (such as Aim, Auto Fire, Point Blank Shot, Suppressing Fire, or Spray). In the app this bonus is automatically calculated into the attack roll.

Laser sights are most commonly a removable attachment under the barrel of a firearm. Laser sights cannot typically be used with weapons that are rolled with Ballistics, however, some artifact weapons contain advanced holographic projection sights that produce the same benefit as a laser sight.

Mounted

This weapon is large, heavy and cannot be properly used without it's specifically designed bipod, monopod, mechanical mount, or other support. Using this weapon unsupported increases the speed cost of any action done with it to 8 and converts all automatic successes in it's attack roll to dice. While unmounted the weapon can only do basic attacks and no special attack actions (such as Aim, Auto Fire, Point Blank Shot, Suppressing Fire, or Spray).

If the weapon requires a mechanical mount it can only count as being supported if you are in a space with the original mechanical mount. If the support is a bipod or monopod you must either be prone or adjacent to a structure you can support the weapon on for the weapon to count as supported.

If you have ranks of the style Super Strength you can ignore this trait.

Sawn

This weapon has had it's barrel permanently sawn off. Modifying a weapon this way reduces the range by 2 and allows you to use the Point Blank Shot action and potentially improves the accuracy of this action, but does not allow you to use the grappling Point Blank Attack action.

Only single shot or simple double barreled shotguns can be modified to gain this trait. Sawing the barrel off other shotguns may improve their concealability or decrease their range, but it will not grant them this trait.

Scope

Scopes make aiming at long range more effective. Any time you use the aim action with a scoped weapon the weapon is considered to have an infinite range.

Scopes are typically a removable attachment above the barrel of a firearm. Weapons rolled with Ballistics typically require scopes specifically designed for the purpose.

Shells

This weapon can use either slug or buckshot ammunition. The normal rules apply for slug ammo, however, when firing buckshot the effects of the weapon's range stat apply to the damage roll instead of the attack roll (meaning all that when the target is out of range all automatic successes on the damage roll are treated as dice instead).

Silencer

Dampens the sound of firing. At close range a silenced firearm can can still be heard without a roll, but at longer distances a silenced firearm requires a Perception + Alertness roll to hear. The GM's discretion will determine what distance the silencer becomes effective at. Weapons that deal greater damage can be heard from greater distances.

Silencers are typically removable attachments to the end of a firearm's barrel that significantly increase the size and difficulty of concealing the weapon. A silenced weapon cannot be used with the Point Blank Shot action, even if the weapon also has the Small trait.

Slow Load

Reloading this weapon has a speed cost of 8 instead of the normal speed cost of 6.

This property is common on revolvers or other weapons that require manually loading multiple individual bullets, but it can be negated by the using a prepared speed load cylinders each time you reload.

Melee Weapon Traits

Reach

With this weapon you can target enemies in any of the spaces that are adjacent to the spaces you could normally target, but doing so increases the speed cost of the attack by 1. If you have the style Zone of Denial anything within the extended range this trait grants is considered adjacent for the purposes of creating the zone.

Spears and other polearms are the most common weapon with this trait, and they tend to have a higher speed cost than weapons with a similar damage roll.

Soft

A target dealt damage by a weapon with this trait may make Stamina roll. Each success on this roll reduces the damage dealt by a point. All unarmed attacks have this trait.

Trip

When using the Trip action with this weapon you may use your normal attack roll (instead of the roll described in the trip action) against the target's Agility + Athletics roll results instead of the sum of those scores.

Armor

Each bodily location can have a different amount of armor on it. For each point of armor you have on a location subtract 1 point of damage from every attack that hits that location.

Armor Protection Examples
Light 1 Leather, thick padding, heavy jacket, concealable kevlar body armor
Medium 2 Unconcealable modern cloth body armor
Heavy 3 Tactical armor with trauma plates or archaic metal armor
Penalties:

Heavy armor has an associated penalty due to its bulk that applies any time you are wearing the armor. Different bodily locations have different penalties listed below. Agility and speed penalties from armor cannot lower your total speed below 3.

Location Penalty
1: Legs 2 points of armor reduces your Speed by 1 point, a third point of armor reduces Speed by 2 points.
2: Off Arm 3 points of armor reduces your Finesse by 1 for all actions using this arm.
3: Main Arm 3 points of armor reduces your Finesse by 1 for all actions using this arm.
4: Lower Body 3 points of armor reduces your Agility rolls by 1 point.
5: Upper Body 3 points of armor reduces your Perception rolls by 1 point.

Artifact Basics

At some point in the past a worldline incursion occurred that ultimately resulted in a small number of artifacts of advanced technology being left behind on our world. These artifacts were most likely brought here and used by whatever beings entered our world during the incursion. These artifacts his since dispersed and ended up either lost or in the hands of various people around the world. Artifacts are rare and highly sought after by those that know of their existence. Artifacts can enhance a character's abilities with brain computer interfaces, advanced material science, and Yau based capabilities. The auxiliary style function(Primary) and its offshoots allow you to build your own artifacts or upgrade existing ones.

There are three types of artifacts; equipment, casts, and components. Equipment refers to weapons and garments, casts are pieces of nano-technology that permanently integrate with their user's body, and components are small items that can be held in the hand, attached to clothing or items, or attached to a cast.

The properties an item can have are sorted into primary functions. Each primary function can host up to 3 secondary functions. Items can only have secondary functions that are hosted by their primary function. Items typically have 1 primary function and 1 or 2 of its related secondary functions, however, some rare casts may have multiple primary functions and several secondary functions.

The functions an item has and method needed to use them is not always readily apparent. Unless you examine an artifact using the Intelligence specialty style, Analyze Cascade, you will not know exactly what functions the artifact has. A simply designed artifact may have a clearly discernable method of activation, but with many artifacts even the method needed to activate or use the artifact cannot be know without using Analyze Cascade.

Creating Artifacts

The auxiliary style function(Primary), function(Cast), and function(Secondary) allow you to build new items with artifact functions or upgrade existing ones. The number of ranks you have in these styles determines how many ranks and functions you can add to an item. To create an item, first you must design it and choose all of the functionality ranks the item will have when finished. Each rank costs approximately $1000 worth of supplies and adds 6 to the number successes you need to amass to finish creating the item (items with the component form factor cost half as much; $500 worth of materials and 3 successes per function).

While not generally illegal, the raw materials needed are typically not easy to acquire. It may take its own separate Hardware or Science roll or some contacts to find an appropriate source for the rare earth metals, circuit etching equipment, and microelectronic components needed. Adding artifact functionality to mundane items also requires the appropriate tools and materials needed to work with items of that type. For example, if you are adding functions to a firearm you will need the appropriate gunsmithing tools and knowledge or if you are making a cast or a component you will need computer science knowledge and hardware manufacting equipment.

Creating an item is an extended action with an interval of 1 week and a roll of Intelligence + Hardware. In order to get a roll for an interval you must spend the majority of your time (about 8 hours a day) that week working on the item. Each interval you work on an item grants you one roll, add the successes of this roll to all previous successes you've rolled on this item. Once you've reached the target number of successes (6 per rank of functionality) the item is complete. Once the item is complete you can opt to take extra intervals rolling Intelligence + Hardware to improve the item's appearance, quality, and reduce its bulk.

During an interval week your Entanglement is devoted entirely to the project, as soon as you start working that week your Entanglement drops to 0 and doesn't begin to regenerate until you stop working on the item. You can stop working on the item for weeks in a row without losing the progress you've made on it, just remember (or write down)how many successes you have so far. If you have to stop working in the middle of an interval you do not get to roll for that interval.

Artifact Functions

Form Factor: Cast

On the most basic level casts are interfaces connecting the mind and body to artifacts or mundane technology. Casts are worn on the body, typically the on the arm but occasionally elsewhere. Once a cast is put on for the first time it bonds to its host and develops connections to the nervous system allowing the wearer to consciously control the cast's functionality as well as any artifact technology attached to it. Once bonded to a host, the abilities granted by a cast are permanent until the cast is either surgically removed or the body part hosting the cast is completely severed or destroyed. A cast you are carrying but not bonded to does not provide you any of its benefits. Attempting to bond with a second cast will leave the first cast attached to the body but completely disable all of its functions. Unlike components and equipment, a single cast can potentially have multiple primary functions.

The way the cast physically integrates itself into its owner's body depends on the quality of the cast's construction and can vary greatly. A small well integrated cast may be completely invisible, or it could appear as nothing more than a tattoo, while a larger cast might appear as a bulky metal object. Some casts even have the ability to recede into their owner's body and become completely hidden or visible on command.

A cast also enables its wearer to utilize all component form factor technologies. In order to use a component it is attached to the cast and activated. Some simple casts have no primary functionality and serve only to interface with components, while some are powerful and contain multiple primary and secondary functions.

Some secondary cast functions can be temporarily integrated into components with the primary function program( ). This allows the program to emulate the functions of the cast in unique ways. If a function has this capability it will be listed with the functions description under the header Program Integration. Casts can have the following primary functions: noo-interface, bio-interface, or digital interface.

Primary Function: noo_interface( )

Hosted Secondary Functions: augment( ), connect_to( ), inhibit( )

This function forges a strong connection between the cast and its wearer's mind as well as creating pathways for the wearer's mind to interface with the minds of others.

Rank 1: Your character can never be the target of any style or artifact that reads or influences minds.

Rank 2: If you concentrate on it you can tell the approximate location of any mind within 9 meters of you. You also have a rough impression of the type of any mind this close to you, be it human, animal, artificial, or otherwise.

Rank 3: If you concentrate on it you can tell approximate location of any mind within 30 meters of you. You also know the current emotional state and mental attributes (Intelligence, Wits, Charisma, Perception and Willpower) of any mind within 9 meters of you.

augment( )

Wearing a cast with this function can augment your mental attributes by digitally supplementing your memory, creating an empathic connection with others, or auto-regulating brain chemistry. Each rank of this style improves either Intelligence, Willpower, or Charisma by one. Typically the choice of the which attribute to improve is hard wired into the cast, but some casts are capable of allowing the person wearing the cast to choose. In either case the choice is permanent.

connect_to( )

Treat each rank of this function the same as a rank in the auxiliary style Ally Communication, however this function does not use an Entanglement point to activate. Ranks in this function stack with ranks in the style. If you have a total of 4 ranks in both this function and that style the range of the effects extend to 100 kilometers. If you have a total of 5 ranks in both this function and the style the range of the effects extend to continental. If you have a total of 6 ranks in both this function and the style the range of the effects extend to global.

Program Integration: A program can emulate connect_to( ) to act as a mental surveillance bug. When placed on a target willing or unwilling person's body or clothing this program allows the cast's owner to hear the entire internal monologue of the target. If placed in a location the cast's owner can feel the basic surface thoughts and emotions of anyone that passes within a meter of the program. Each point of Entanglement stored in the program allows it to function this way for 3 hours.

inhibit( )

This function lets you jam communications between others in a local area by inhibiting the language processing capabilities of their brain. When fully developed it can also potentially inhibit signals from propagating between regions of a brain, reducing mental faculties of target.

Rank 1: As a standard action or a combat action with a speed cost of 2 you can spend a point of Entanglement to jam language between people within 3 meters of you for the rest of the scene. You are not effected, but any allies in the affected area are. People within the effected area experience a greatly increased difficulty with comprehending and paying attention to language that comes to them via speech, recordings, text, or telethesia. People thus effected must roll Willpower + Guts against a difficulty equal to the results of a single roll of your Charisma + Manipulation, failing this roll means that the person failing cannot comprehend any language. Ability to speak and write are not inhibited, only the ability to comprehend, as such playing back audio recorded in the effected area later would sound like normal sensible speech.

Rank 2: As a standard action or a combat action with a speed cost of 2 you can spend a point of Entanglement to jam language between people within a number of meters equal to your Charisma + 3 for the rest of the scene. You and any individuals in the effected area that you chose are not effected. Anyone effected area cannot form coherent spoken, written, or telepathic messages and cannot comprehend speech, recordings, text, or telethesia. There is no roll to resist this effect. Playing back recordings of people attempting to speak in the effected area would sound like gibberish.

Rank 3: As a standard action or a combat action with a speed cost of 2 you can spend a point of Entanglement to inhibit signal propagation in a single target's brain for the rest of the scene. A target cannot roll to resist this effect, and once effected will have their natural Wits and Intelligence scores reduced to 0. This only reduces natural attribute scores, attribute points granted by styles are not reduced by this function.

Program Integration: A program emulating inhibit acts as a communications jamming device that can be placed anywhere. A point of entanglement stored in the program allows it to act as per rank 1 or rank 2(if the cast is capable of rank 2) of this function for an hour once activated. The effected area is 3 meters centered on wherever the program is planted and activated. The duration and effected area can be increased by storing additional Entanglement at a rate of 1 hour or 3 meters per point.

Primary Function: bio_interface( )

Hosted Secondary Functions: augment( ), assemble( ), exo_supply( )

This function allows a cast to connect with its owner's physiology as well as other living things.

Rank 1: If you concentrate on it you can tell the approximate location of any living organism within 9 meters of you. You also have a rough impression the size and complexity of the organisms this close.

Rank 2: If you concentrate on it you can tell the approximate location of any living organism within 30 meters of you. You also know the health, species and physical attributes (Strength, Finesse, Agility, Stamina, and Perception) of any organism within 9 meters of you.

Rank 3: You are completely immune to the detrimental effects of poisons and diseases and while you require normal amounts of nourishment and sleep to remain both alive and comfortable, your performance is no longer negatively impacted by a lack of either.

augment( )

This cast can increase certain attributes by increasing the power of sensory organs, increasing the efficiency of the body's use of resources, or speeding up the brain's raw processing power. Each rank of this style improves Perception, Stamina, or Wits by 1 point. Typically the choice of the which attribute to improve is hard wired into the cast, but some casts are capable of allowing the person wearing the cast to choose. In either case the choice is permanent.

assemble( )

This function allows the cast operate as a nano-assembler of biological materials for the host body. It can rewrite its wearer's genetics, as well as speed healing, immune processes, and cell division. It can even allow the wearer to change the body's physical appearance.

Rank 1: You heal 1 point of damage on all locations every day.

Rank 2: You heal 2 points of damage on all locations every day. You can make minor changes to your appearance (including facial features, bone structure, body shape) over the course of 24 hours.

Rank 3: You heal 3 points of damage on all locations every day. Given 24 hours you can make drastic changes to your appearance and even perfectly mirror another person. When disguised as another person no one will suspect you are an impostor unless you act in some way out of character for that person.

Program Integration: A program emulating Bio-Assembly can accelerate healing in the person carrying it to the same rate provided by ranks in this function for 1 day per point of Entanglement stored in the program. Alternately this program could be set to change the appearance of anyone that activates it to predetermined parameters.

exo_supply( )

This function allows you to pull raw materials from the Yau into your body in order to survive in times of need. These materials are transfused directly into your bloodstream and because of this they cannot be shared with anyone else.

Rank 1: You no longer require air to breath. You can pull enough air out of ambient materials in the Yau to sustain yourself indefinitely with no ill effects. While you no longer require air, you are still more comfortable breathing than not, your aerobic capacity remains the same as before, and you gain no additional protection from extreme pressure or vacuum.

Rank 2: You can create enough water from ambient materials in the Yau to sustain yourself without drinking indefinitely with no ill effects. This function now also can automatically gather a supply of nutrients and store them in the Yau for later use. While this does not allow you to live indefinitely without food, it does triple the amount of time you can go with limited or no nutrition. You can go with no food whatsoever for a total of three months before you begin to suffer any ill effects. If you use a significant portion of your nutrient reserve it takes approximately a week to fully replenish.

Rank 3: You can store a supply of your own blood and blood components in the Yau. This supply can be used for either emergency blood transfusions or for blood doping. Either use depletes the supply of blood. It takes 1 week after blood doping and 2 weeks after an emergency transfusion for your body to regenerate the supply of blood. The emergency transfusion activates any time you fall unconscious due to blood loss or, as a full action, any time you choose. When the transfusion activates you immediately stabilize and stop losing regeneration due to blood loss. If you have lost any points of regeneration from injury or blood loss, you will regain one point immediately and another point at the start of each round. Alternatively, you can use the red blood cells from your supply for blood doping. For the next 24 hours after you blood dope the interval length of all exhaustion checks is doubled.

Program Integration: A program emulating exo_supply( ) acts a source of nourishment for anyone carrying it. Each point of Entanglement stored in the program can act as three days worth of food, water, or air; or one day of all three .

Primary Function: cpu_interface( )

Hosted Secondary Functions: exo_drive( ), exo_cortex( ), simulation( )

This function allows a cast's owner to mentally interface with and control computers. This function does not allow you to bypass any passwords or security unless you already know how to hack through them using normal means.

Rank 1: You can concentrate in order to locate the approximate positions of any active computer components or artifact technology within 30 meters. You can also identify the all of the primary functions on any artifact technology.

Rank 2: You can mentally control any computer in an adjacent space without touching any input devices or seeing any output devices.

Rank 3: You can mentally control any computer you can see or within 30 meters without touching any input devices or seeing any output devices. Any computer networked with one of these computers can also be controlled. You can also identify the all of the secondary functions on any artifact technology.

drive( )

This function allows a cast to digitally capture and store all sensory data its wearer experiences. The wearer can review this data at a later time. The wearer can chose the level of detail to review, anywhere from completely reliving the events to simply reading a single word that was seen earlier.

This cast can interface with a computer to store large amounts traditional data, or copy its sensory data to a computer or another cast. Each day of sensory data roughly equivalent to a petabyte of disk space.

Rank 1: The cast stores all data experienced in the last day.

Rank 2: The cast stores all data experienced in the last month.

Rank 3: The cast stores all data experienced in the last year. This cast will also automatically make a complete backup copy of your mind. While this backup copy is not immediately useful to you, certain circumstances or other advanced technologies may make it possible for your backed up mind to find a new body in the event of your death. If someone finds and bonds with your cast after your death they may reactivate your store conciousness allowing you to communicate with the new cast owner.

Program Integration: A program can emulate exo_drive( ) to act as a surveillance bug. If placed in a location it stores audio and 360 degrees of video. If placed on a computer it records all data coming in and out, including network traffic, key logs, and all video output. If placed on a person's body or clothing it records all sensory data. The bug must either be recovered or attached to a wireless transmitter to recover this data. Each point of Entanglement stored in the bug allows it to store a day's worth of data.

cortex( )

This function allows the cast to manufacture powerful sub-nanoscale computing resources and store them in the Yau.

Rank 1: Your cast can supplement any computer you use, making it as powerful as the world's top supercomputers.

Rank 2: If you wish, you can instantly overload any computer, making it completely unusable until manually rebooted, just by touching it.

Rank 3: If you wish, you can instantly overload an entire computer network or a piece of artifact technology, making it completely unusable until manually rebooted, just by touching it. Touching a contested item may require a grapple roll if the target knows what you are trying to do. Rebooting an artifact item takes about a minute. Disabled equipment retains its mundane properties (example: a firearm that loses its enhanced properties is still a working firearm).

Program Integration: A program can emulate exo_cortex( ) in order to crash a computer system or artifact it is attached to when a specific pre-established condition is met.

simulation( )

This function allows the wearer to access the mysterious abandoned digital simulation of reality that exists parallel to most populated areas on Earth. Entering the simulation is similar to entering the Yau and is subject to the same rules, see the arcane style class Sublimation for more details. Entering the simulation leaves your unconscious body behind in normal space unless you also have the styles required to bring your body into the Yau.

Rank 1: You can access this simulation and view the events that are happening in it at your current location on Earth.

Rank 2: You can enter the simulation, move around in it and interact with it as a digital avatar.

Rank 3: You can alter simulation's basic programming. This allows you almost unlimited freedom to change both your avatar and its virtual surroundings.

Form Factor: Component

Components are small objects, typically about the size of a coin, that can be carried in pockets, worn attached to clothing, or attached to a cast. Components are also cheaper and simpler to make than other technology. Each rank of a component's functions requires only 3 successes and $500 worth of materials to create.

With a few exceptions, components can only be used by characters that have either a cast or ranks in the arcane style class function(Primary).

Primary Function: aperture( )

Hosted Secondary Functions: index( ), range( ), volume( )

Aperture components are typically used by being attached to a cast, but can also be used by being held in the pocket of someone that has the style function(Primary).

An aperture component allows you to hide an item in the Yau and recall it later. Only small, non-living, unattended objects that you can touch can be moved to the Yau. Storing or removing an object takes approximately a minute.

The items stored with an aperture component stay with the component, not with you. Anyone that can use this component could extract any items you have stored in it. If multiple aperture components are in the same 1 cubic meter space only the one with the most function ranks is operable, any others cannot be used to store or retrieve items.

Rank 1: The Yau aperture is 3 cm. You can store one object that can fit though a 3 cm hole.

Rank 2: The Yau aperture is 9 cm. You can store one object that can fit though a 9 cm hole.

Rank 3: The Yau aperture is 30 cm. You can store one object that can fit though a 30 cm hole.

index( )

By storing and indexing objects in an organized fashion, this function decreases the amount of time it takes to store or recall an object.

Rank 1: Storing or recalling an object is a full round action with a speed cost of 3.

Rank 2: Storing or recalling an object is a combat action with a speed cost of 2.

Rank 3: Storing or recalling an object is a free action.

range( )

A basic aperture component can only store objects you can touch; this function allows you to do it at a distance.

Rank 1: This component can store any unattended object that is within 9 cm of it.

Rank 2: This component can store any unattended object that is within the same space as it.

Rank 3: This component can store any unattended object that is in an adjacent space.

volume( )

This function increases the number of objects that can be stored. Items still must have at least one dimension that is smaller than that dictated by the host function.

Rank 1: This component can store any number of objects that will fit within an empty space of 30 cubic cm.

Rank 2: This component can store any number of objects that will fit within an empty space of 1 cubic meter.

Rank 3: This component can store any number of objects that will fit within an empty space of 3 cubic meters.

Primary Function: program( )

Hosted Secondary Functions: capacity( ), complexity, usability( )

Programs are small components that can be used to store Entanglement points or emulate styles by expending stored Entanglement.

The most basic use of a program is as an Entanglement battery. Storing Entanglement points in the program takes a few minutes of concentration. If you are carrying a program with Entanglement stored in it you can use the Entanglement stored there to activate styles just like spending normal Entanglement points.

Programs can also be used to emulate a specific style. Setting a program to emulate a style takes a few minutes of concentration. Activating a program is a combat action costing 1 speed and acts the same as activating the style the program is emulating. Programs can only emulate styles that you possess that have Entanglement activation costs, they cannot emulate any passive style. Typically they are used to emulate arcane styles or activate technique. The person setting the program chooses what style will be used and decides any of the specifics about what the style will do, but the person activating the style chooses what the program will target (only things the style can normally target can be selected). A program can be reset to emulate a different style at any point, but doing so expends any Entanglement currently stored in it.

Programs can also be used to emulate certain functions of cast artifacts. If a cast function can be emulated, its description will contain a section labelled Program Integration that describes how a program can use that function.

You can carry multiple programs at once, however, if you use more points of Entanglement from a program than your maximum Entanglement score within the course of an hour you will develop Crisis Sickness(see chapter 3 for details) for the next 24 hours.

Rank 1: This program can store 1 total point of Entanglement and can only function as a Entanglement battery.

Rank 2: This program can store 2 total points of Entanglement and can only function as a Entanglement battery.

Rank 3: This program can store 3 total points of Entanglement and can function as a Entanglement battery or emulate any cast's program integration functions.

capacity( )

Increases the amount of Entanglement a program can store. This stacks with the host function.

Rank 1: This program can store 3 additional points of Entanglement.

Rank 2: This program can store 3 additional points of Entanglement.

Rank 3: This program can store 3 additional points of Entanglement.

complexity( )

This function allows higher tiers of styles to be stored and used.

Rank 1: This program emulate any tier 1 style.

Rank 2: This program can emulate any tier 2 style.

Rank 3: This program can emulate any tier 3 style.

usability( )

Typically a program can only be used by its creator or by the owner of a cast it is attached to, this function allows it to be used by others.

Rank 1: Program can be used by anyone with the same styles that are emulated by it.

Rank 2: Program can be used by anyone with styles.

Rank 3: Program can be used by anyone.

Primary Function: shield( )

Hosted Secondary Functions: durability( ), recharge( ), usability( )

Shields are components that are typically attached to clothing or a cast that envelop the wearer in a protective field.

If someone hits a shielded person with an attack they don't need to roll location, instead they deal damage to the shield. Each point of damage dealt to a shield reduces its durability by 1. Once the durability reaches 0 the shield is depleted and future attacks affect the shielded charact normally until the shield recharges. An attack that depletes a shield is still completely deflected, even if it does much more damage than the shield's current durability. A damaged or depleted shield recharges at a rate of 1 point of durability per hour.

Attempting to grapple a shielded person functions normally. If the grapple is successful both parties in the grapple will be protected from outside attacks but can damage each other normally.

If multiple shield components are in the same 1 meter of space, only the one with the most function ranks is operable. Even if the operable shield is depleted no others can function in the same space as it.

Rank 1: Base shield durability is 1.

Rank 2: Base shield durability is 2.

Rank 3: Base shield durability is 3.

durability( )

Increases the protection a shield provides. This stacks with the base durability provided by the host function. This function also conveys increased resistance to ranged attacks, however damage dealt by either the Auto Fire or Point Blank Shot actions ignores this resistance.

Rank 1: Shield durability increases by 1. Any ranged attack that damages this shield does 1 less damage than normal.

Rank 2: Shield durability increases by 1. Any ranged attack that damages this shield does 2 less damage than normal.

Rank 3: Shield durability increases by 1. Any ranged attack that damages this shield does 3 less damage than normal.

recharge( )

Typically a shield regains a point of lost durability each hour, this function increases that rate.

Rank 1: Shield recharges one point of lost durability every minute.

Rank 2: Shield recharges one point of lost durability every 3 rounds.

Rank 3: Shield recharges one point of lost durability every round.

usability( )

Without this function, shields can only be used when attached to a cast or used by someone with the style function(Primary). With it, they can be used by others.
Rank 1: Shield can be used by anyone with styles.
Rank 2: Shield can be used by anyone.
Rank 3: Shield can be recharged quickly by anyone with Entanglement. Once per round a character can use a point of Entanglement to restore a point of durability to the shield. Doing so is a free action.

Form Factor: Equipment

Weapons and armor can be enhanced by artifact technology to be increasingly effective. Enhanced equipment can typically be used by anyone. Any piece of enhanced equipment has its mundane statistics (armor rating, damage, range etc.) plus its enhanced functions. If a piece of equipment ever has its functions disabled it still acts as a mundane version of that piece of equipment.

Primary Function: firearm( )

Hosted Functions: power( ), range( ), supply( )

Ranged weapons can be modified to deal increased damage, have increased range, or store ammunition in the Yau.

Rank 1: This weapon has reduced weight, recoil, and is easily wielded, increase all attack rolls made with it by 1 die. It's form can be stylized to take on the appearance of almost any similarly sized and shaped weapon permanently. This weapon cannot be damaged by conventional means.

Rank 2: The weapon deals an additional die of damage than it normally would.

Rank 3: The speed cost to fire this weapon decreases by 1 (down to a minimum of 2).

power( )

Each rank of this function increases the damage roll for this weapon by 1 die.

range( )

This function increases the range of a firearm. Each rank adds 3 to the weapon's range stat.

supply( )

This function stores ammunition in the Yau, decreasing or eliminating the need to reload. Shell casings are still be expelled as usual.

Rank 1: The gun's effective clip size is equal to however many bullets you have acquired and stored in the Yau, up to a limit of 99 bullets.

Rank 2: This gun can manufacture its own bullets and store them in the Yau. It will never run out of ammo.

Rank 3: As a free action, this gun can be stored in the Yau or quickly recalled from the Yau and into its owner's hand.

Primary Function: melee( )

Hosted Functions: edge( ), phase( ), store( )

Melee weapons can be modified to deal increased damage, phase through armor, or be stored in the Yau.

Rank 1: The lighter weight of this weapon reduces its speed cost by 1 (down to a minimum of 2). This weapon's form can be stylized to take on the appearance of almost any similarly sized and shaped weapon permanently. This weapon cannot be damaged by conventional means.

Rank 2: Increase attack rolls with this weapon by 1 die.

Rank 3: The damage dealt by this weapon increases by 1 die.

edge( )

Each rank of this function increases the damage roll for this weapon by 1 success.

phase( )

This function causes a weapon to phase in and out of the Yau in precise, calculated ways that allow it to pass through armor. Ignore 1 point of your target's armor for each rank of this function that the weapon you are using has.

store( )

Weapons with this functionality can be stored in the Yau and quickly recalled into their owner's hand.

Rank 1: Storing or recalling this weapon is a full action with a speed cost of 3.

Rank 2: Storing or recalling this weapon is a combat action with a speed cost of 1.

Rank 3: Storing or recalling this weapon is a free action.

Primary Function: garment( )

Hosted Functions: augment( ), harden( ), store( )

Garments can be enhanced to provide more protection or increase one of the wearer's attributes. Garment functions can be applied to garments that cover one body location or to entire outfits. Any armor bonus they provide only protects the locations they are worn on.

When determining the time (but not the material cost) required to create a garment, treat it as 1 rank higher than normal for each location it covers. For example, a garment with 6 ranks worth of functions that covers 3 locations would cost as much as a garment with 9 ranks worth of functions that covers one location.

Rank 1: This garment is highly durable and cannot be damaged by conventional means.

Rank 2: The form and appearance of this garment can be stylized to take on the appearance of almost any garment permanently. For example, a garment with the properties of a flak vest could be made to look like a t-shirt or an archaic breast plate.

Rank 3: This garment is lighter weight than normal and provides no armor penalty.

augment( )

When this artifact is created one of Strength, Finesse, or Agility is chosen and each rank of this function increases the chosen attribute by 1 point while the garment is worn.

If a garment with the augment function is designed to cover multiple bodily locations, all of the locations must be worn for the effects of this function to apply. If you are wearing multiple different garments that have this function, only the one with the most ranks in this function provides a bonus. If multiple different garments with the same rank of this function are wore, only the one closest to the Upper Body provides a bonus.

harden( )

Each rank of this function increases the armor rating of this garment by 1 without increasing the bulk or penalties of this garment.

store( )

Garments or sets of garments with this functionality can be stored in the Yau and quickly recalled into an equipped and active position.

Rank 1: Storing or recalling this garment or set of garments is a full action with a speed cost of 6.

Rank 2: Storing or recalling this garment or set of garments is a combat action with a speed cost of 3.

Rank 3: Storing or recalling this garment is a free action.
