Action Types in Combat

Combat Action

Most basic actions during a fight are combat actions. You can do a single combat action each time it is your turn, but you can take multiple combat actions during a single round if you have enough speed for each action.

Combat actions have an associated speed cost, when you attempt a combat action deduct the speed cost from your current speed. In one round you can do any number of combat actions with total speed cost that is less than your current speed value. Each combat action takes an entire turn in the round.

If a combat action does not result in movement you can move 1 space on normal terrain either just before or just after you do a combat action, doing so does not increase the speed cost of the action.

If it is your first turn in a round and you do not have enough speed to execute a specific combat action you can convert it into a full action with a speed cost of your current speed total.

Move Action

Move actions typically move your character around in combat. They have an associated speed cost per space moved. Multiple move actions can be done in any combination and in any order on your turn, for example you could start running, jump over something, and then continue running in one turn.

Defensive Action

Defensive actions do not happen on your turn. Instead, you can declare a defensive action anytime you are attacked. The speed cost of the defensive action is deducted when you roll the action. The only limit on the number of defensive actions you can take in a round is your speed. During any defensive action you can move 1 space into normal terrain either just before or just after a defensive action happens, doing so does not increase the speed cost of the action.

Unlike combat actions that can be converted to full actions if you do not have enough speed, you can only use a defensive action if you have enough speed. If a defensive action does not result in movement, you can move a space as a part of the action with no additional speed cost.

Certain circumstances may deny you the ability to take defensive actions. Any time you are unable to use defensive actions, you are also able to be targeted by the Stealth Attack action.

Full Action

Full actions are treated just like combat actions that take longer. You can only do full actions on your first turn in a round and this action prevents you from doing other actions in the round.

On any full action that does not cause you to move, you can move a single space on normal terrain either just before or just after the full action happens, doing so does not increase the speed cost of the action.

Extended Action

Extended actions are used when you wish to spend extra time and effort on an action that would be too difficult to do in a single turn or round. Any action can be converted to an extended action to represent extra time and effort.

When doing an extended action the GM will set a target number of successes and an interval time. The interval time is determined by how long the action takes to do, a turn or a round for actions in combat, a minute for things that take little time, an hour for actions that take a long time, or even a day or week for long term projects. You roll once every interval, adding all the successes from all previous intervals to the current roll. You can stop at any time and the total number of successes rolled in every interval indicates how well the action was performed. If an extended action is stopped and then resumed later, the GM will decide if the previous successes rolled can continue to apply or if you must start over from scratch (for example, trying to do something usually requires starting over while trying to make something will allow you to pick up where you left off).

When using extended actions in combat deduct the speed cost of the action every interval, or if the speed cost is greater than your current speed reduce your speed to 0 and increase the interval time to a full round (if it wasn't already). Each interval you can move 1 space as a part of the extended action, however, some tasks might require you to stay stationary in order to complete them.

Free Action

A free action is anything you can do in a split second. Free actions often have no speed cost and multiple free actions can be done in a turn. You can do a free action at any time, even if it is not your turn. Free actions do not allow you to move a space while executing them.

Basic Combat Actions

Basic Attack
Combat Action - Variable Speed Cost

This action has three parts, the attack roll to determine if it hits, the location roll to see where it hits, and a damage roll to see how hard it hits.

The attack roll for close combat attacks is Finesse + Melee or Strength + Unarmed. The attack roll for ranged attacks is Perception + either Firearms or Ballistics. This roll only needs to tie your target's passive defense in order to hit, unless the target makes a dodge roll, in which case you need to beat this roll by at least a success to in order to hit. If you miss, the action ends now with no effect other than deducting its normal speed cost.

For the location roll a single d6. Numbers 1-5 indicate the bodily location hit and rolling a 6 indicates a critical hit. On a critical hit reroll the die to determine where the critical hit lands, if you roll a 6 again keep rerolling until you roll a location between 1 and 5. Each time you rolled a 6 on the location roll increases the critical hit level by 1.

Each extra success you get on the attack roll above the number you needed to hit grants you an adjustment to the location roll. Each adjustment lets you move the location hit to an adjacent location, and each remaining adjustment after moving to the desired location increases the critical level by 1.

The damage roll of a melee attack is Strength + the weapon's damage stat. The damage roll for a ranged weapon is determined entirely by the weapon's damage stat. The total amount of damage done is equal to the number of successes rolled in the damage roll plus the critical level.

The speed cost of an attack is determined by weapon used. Unarmed attacks cost 2 speed while heavier weapons require more.

Ranged weapons also have a range. The attack roll on any attack within this range is treated normally and the attack roll for anything outside of that range is rolled with the disadvantage of having all automatic successes replaced with dice.

Any attack made using your off hand (this might happen if your main hand is disabled or if you are dual wielding) is rolled with the disadvantage of having all automatic successes replaced with dice. If you are using your off hand to attack a target outside of your weapon's range replace all automatic successes with dice and don't add the dice from your attributes to the attack roll.

Dodge
Defensive Action - Speed Cost: 3

Anytime you are the target of an attack you can choose if you want to attempt to dodge the attack or not. A dodge attempt increases your chances of avoiding the attack but has a speed cost. Unlike combat actions which must be done on your turn in the initiative order, dodging is a defensive action that can be done anytime that you are the target of an attack.

Normally when you are the target of an attack the attacker must only tie your passive defense to hit you. When you dodge the attacker must instead beat the number of successes you get on your dodge roll by at least 1.

The dodge roll for melee attacks is Agility + Defense. The dodge roll for ranged attacks is Wits + Defense.

Draw Weapon
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 3

The circumstances at the start of the fight determine whether you have your weapons in hand or stowed. This action must be done before a sheathed weapon can be used. This action can also be used to put a weapon away or switch to a different weapon (switching from one weapon to another only takes a single draw action). Dropping a weapon to the ground instead of putting it away is a free action.

Move
Move Action - Speed Cost: 1 per space

To run from one location to another spend a single point of speed for each space crossed.

You may combine this action with other move actions, such as Traverse, on a single turn.

Reload
Combat Action - Speed cost: 6

Use this action to reload an empty weapon. This action assumes that you are carrying ammunition that is easily accessible in a small pocket, belt, or bandolier. If the ammunition is stashed in a bag or some other container on your person reloading becomes a full action.

Rest
Full Action - Speed Cost: none

When using this action you can do no more than walk a single space this round. After this using this action you will regain twice as much speed as normal at the start of the next round.

If you don't have any bodily locations that are damaged above your Wound Max, but you have lost some Endurance, resting will also restore a lost point Endurance.

Special Move Actions

Sprint
Full Action - Speed Cost: current speed total

Spend all of your current speed and move any number of spaces up to double the amount of speed you spent.

Sprinting is a full action, not a move action. As such, it can only be done on your first turn in a round.

Traverse
Move Action - Speed Cost: 2 per space

Difficult terrain, such as rubble, narrow ledges, vertical surfaces, deep water, or long jumps requires the Traverse action for you to balance, climb, jump, or swim through it. Any time you cross a space with the Traverse action, you must spend 2 points of speed instead of just one.

If the terrain is only moderately difficult you can simply cross it and spend the required speed. However, if the terrain is difficult enough that crossing it poses a risk of danger, such as a narrow ledge, a slippery climb, or rough water, you are limited to crossing it a rate of 1 space per round. If you wish to move faster than this you must roll Agility + Athletics. Each success you roll lets you cross a single difficult space this turn. An exceptionally difficult surface, such as a tightrope or jagged icy rocks, requires 2 successes for each space crossed.

Rolling no successes means you cannot move this turn as you struggle for equilibrium. Rolling no successes two turns in a row results in falling or sinking. You can avoid this by not attempting a second balance roll immediately after failing one, and instead only traversing a single space next round.

The consequences of falling or sinking depend on the terrain. You may just be prone and need to make a move action costing 2 speed to stand up, or you may take damage from a long fall, or you may be stuck underwater until you can roll at least 1 success on an Agility + Athletics. Drowning does not typically happen at timescales combat is concerned with, but if you are stuck underwater you must follow the rules regarding drowning in the hazards section of chapter 3.

If the difficult terrain includes jumps longer than a single space (you can always jump over a single space without rolling), you must roll Strength + Athletics. If you have a running start, every success you get adds 1 to the number of spaces you can jump across, if you do not have a running start (or the jump is vertical) every 2 successes raises the number of spaces you can clear by 1. If you do not roll enough successes to clear the gap, you may stop before it instead of falling.

Special Actions

The following section describes several special actions you can take in combat. When any of these action descriptions mention making a normal basic attack or dodge roll, these rolls are treated with all of the benefits of styles or artifact functions that specifically improve attack and dodge rolls. However, if the action description specifies a roll that is not a normal basic attack or dodge roll, this roll does not gain the benefits of any styles or artifact functions that specifically improve attack or dodge rolls.

Aim
Extended Action - Speed Cost: 2 per interval

This action allows you to take extra time to line up a difficult shot by turning a basic ranged attack action into an extended action with an interval of a single turn.

During each interval, you spend aiming make a roll of Perception + Ballistics. Each time you roll add the results to the sum of the results of all previous rolls. You can only use the aim action for a number of intervals in a row up to your Perception score. After aiming, the next basic attack action that you make with the aimed weapon will have its attack roll improved by adding a number of extra successes equal to the total number of successes you accumulated while aiming.

If you use any other action other than continuing to aim or attacking with the aimed weapon, all accumulated aiming successes are lost.

Auto Fire
Full Action - Speed Cost: 8

This action allows you to empty the entire clip of an automatic weapon into a target. Roll a basic attack, but add 9 extra dice to both the attack roll and the damage roll. Roll location and critical levels normally. After rolling damage, divide the total damage dealt evenly between the location hit by the attack and all adjacent locations (round down if the damage does not divide evenly).

For example, if you hit location 5 (upper body) for 8 damage, both it and the adjacent location 4 (lower body) would take 4 damage; or if you hit location 3 (main arm) for 10 damage, it and the adjacent locations 4 (lower body) and 2 (off arm) would all take 3 damage and the remaining 1 point of damage that did not divide evenly wold be ignored.

This action can only be done with weapons that have the Automatic trait and have, at least, three-quarters of its ammo remaining. This action depletes all remaining ammo on normal weapons or half of the ammo of a weapon with the Extended Magazine trait.

Charge
Combat Action - Speed Cost: number of spaces crossed + a melee attack's normal speed cost

This action allows you to move to a target and hit it with a melee attack as a single action. It can only target enemies that are at least 3 spaces away from you but closer than your current speed total and accessible via a straight line over normal terrain. Using this action moves you to the nearest open space adjacent to your target and lets you make a single basic melee attack as part of a single action.

This action has the same speed cost as moving to the target and hitting it normally, but it allows slower characters without enough speed to do so in a single round by converting Charge into a full action.

Defensive Stance
Combat Action - Variable Speed Cost

As a combat action with no speed cost you can adopt a defensive stance until your next turn or until you opt to drop the defensive stance. While in a defensive stance, you can choose how much speed you want to spend on each incoming attack. For each point of speed you spend, treat your passive defense as 1 point higher against that attack.

This action is not treated as a defensive action and as such can only be started on your turn.

Disarm
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 6

Roll Finesse + Unarmed if you are unarmed, or roll Finesse + Melee if you are wielding a melee weapon. If the number of successes you roll is greater than the sum of the target's Strength + Melee scores (this is not a resisted check, you must beat the sum of their scores, not the results of a roll), then the opponent's weapon falls to the floor. Picking a weapon up from the floor is a combat action with a speed cost of 3. If you score 3 successes more than you needed to knock the weapon to the floor, then you can choose whether the opponent's weapon falls to the floor or is in your hands ready for use. If you already have a melee weapon your main hand, the stolen weapon goes into your off hand.

Dual Wield
Combat Action - Variable Speed Cost

If you are wielding a weapon in each hand this action will allow you to attack with both as a single action. Roll a normal basic attack with each weapon, however, the attack roll for both weapons is rolled with the disadvantage of having each automatic success replaced with a die (normally only off hand weapons have this disadvantage and main hand weapons do not, but using both weapons as a single Dual Wield action applies this disadvantage to both).

The speed cost of dual wielding is the same as the total cost of using both weapons as two separate actions, but this action allows you to use them both on one turn or as a full action if you do not have enough speed. During this action, both weapons are considered to have the same range as the weapon with the lower range.

You cannot use this action with any weapon that has Bow, Mounted, or any of the Explosive traits. You can only use this action if both weapons you are holding can reasonably be used with a single hand. For example, wielding two knives or pistols is possible while wielding two sledgehammers or assault rifles is not (however, if you have any ranks of the style Super Strength you can dual wield with any weapon that isn't a Bow, Mounted, or an Explosive). Both weapons must be either both ranged weapons or both melee weapons, you cannot use this action with mix of ranged and melee weapons.

The style Ambidexterity reduces the disadvantage penalties and speed costs of this action.

First Aid
Extended Action - Speed Cost: 6 per interval

First aid can prevent downed characters from bleeding to death. First aid is an extended action with a speed cost of 6 and an interval of a full round. Every 3 successes you get on a roll of Intelligence + Medicine causes the target's endurance to move 1 point closer to normal. Restoring at least a single point of Endurance this way prevents any further Endurance loss due to blood loss until any further damage on a disabled or destroyed location is taken. If you have lost consciousness due to a negative current Endurance, restoring enough Endurance to turn your current Endurance into a positive number will cause you to regain concsiousness at the start of the next round.

The first aid action can also patch up disabled or destroyed body parts. Make an extended first aid check against a target number of 6. Once the number of successes has been met the destroyed body part is treated as disabled or the disabled body part is treated as normal. This does not actually heal any points of damage, it just allows you to use a disabled body part without the penalty associated with disabled or destroyed status. If this restores the use of a location that was causing unconsciousness, the consciousness will be restored at the start of the next round. If this part is damaged again it goes back to being treated as disabled or destroyed.

In order to attempt first aid, you must be carrying the appropriate equipment and be adjacent to the target of the healing. The target must remain in an adjacent space and cannot take standard or defensive actions without interrupting the first aid attempt. You can use a combat action with a speed cost of 2 to roll Wits + Medicine in order to attempt to locate improvised first aid equipment such as cloth for bandages or sticks to use as splints. The number of successes the GM will require for this action to work depends on where you are and severity of the wounds. If you do manage to locate it you'll need to use move actions to move to the improvised supplies, free them from anything they may be attached to, and bring them back to the person in need of first aid.

Point Blank Shot
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 4

Any firearm with the trait Small or the trait Sawn can be used as melee weapons on adjacent targets with devastating effects. The attack roll for this maneuver is Finesse + Melee, however, if the weapon has the trait Sawn you may choose to use either this attack roll or the weapon's normal attack roll. Roll location normally. All dice on the damage roll are replaced with automatic successes.

Spray
Full Action - Speed Cost: 8

When using a weapon with the trait Automatic, you can use this action to spray bullets at up to 6 adjacent targets. Make 1 attack roll with 3 extra dice in it. Compare this roll to each target's passive defense or dodge roll result to see if that target is hit. Roll one location for each target hit. Make one damage roll, every target hit receives this much damage in the location that was hit.

This action can only be done with weapons that have the trait Automatic and that have at least three-quarters of their ammo remaining. This action depletes remaining ammo in normal weapons or half of the ammo in weapons with the Extended Magazine trait.

Stealth Attack
Combat Action - Speed Cost: weapon speed + 2

Any time an opponent is denied the ability to make active dodge attempts against you (this happens if the target is unaware of your presence, is flanked, is in the middle of climbing or traversing rough terrain, or is otherwise unable to defend) you can attempt a stealth attack. When using a stealth attack, add an extra die and an extra success to your damage roll.

When executing a stealth attack with a ranged weapon, you must be within 3 spaces of your opponent.

The auxiliary style Stealth Attacks makes this action more potent.

Suppressing Fire
Full Action - Speed Cost: 8

Use this action to spend the rest of the round emptying a firearms's magazine in one direction. Choose a line starting from your character and going off at any angle, any space within range that this line crosses is a targeted area. If your gun has the Automatic trait, every space adjacent to the normally targeted area is also a targeted area.

Any enemy that enters or ends a turn in a targeted space may immediately be targeted by a normally rolled basic attack as a free action with no additional speed cost. The target can attempt to dodge this free attack, but only if their dodge action moves them into an untargeted space. Enemies behind full cover or enemies that have used the Take Cover action to enter a targeted space are not hit by suppressing fire. You can only make up to 3 free attacks this way with a normal firearm, up to 6 free attacks if the firearm has the Automatic trait, and up to 9 free attacks if it has the Extended Magazine trait.

You can only use this action if your firearm has a magazine of at least 9 shots that is completely full. After using this action your weapon's magazine is depleted, unless it has the Extended Magazine trait, in which case it is halved.

Take Cover
Move Action - Speed Cost: 1

If you are in or adjacent to partial cover, you can use this action to duck behind it. This causes the cover to act as complete cover and all attackers that are on the other side of this cover cannot target you with attacks. You remain protected this way until you move or take another action.

If this is the only action you take in a round it counts as resting, treat it as a full action and then at the start of the next round regain twice as much speed as normal.

Cover only protects you from opponents that it blocks line-of-sight to. An enemy may take actions to move to a new location that your cover does not offer any protection from.

Trip
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 6

Roll Finesse + Unarmed if you are unarmed, or Finesse + Melee if you are using a melee weapon. If you get more successes than the sum of the target's Agility + Athletics scores (this is not a resisted check, you must beat their total score, not the results of a roll), then the target is tripped. This attack does no damage but the target is knocked prone. Standing up again is a move action that costs 3 speed.

Wait
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 1

Wait until a more opportune time to act by spending a single point of speed and then lowering your initiative slot to any lower number in the current turn, or any higher number in the next turn. If other characters also act on that number you decide where you go in that order. This change to your initiative lasts for the rest of combat. You cannot use this action to interrupt another character's action, you may only change your initiative to go just before they do or just after they do. If the only action you take in a round is waiting, you also gain the benefits of the Rest action. For the purposes of determining when a round ends, this action counts as taking no action, as such if everyone either waits or takes no action in a turn the round ends. Like all other combat actions, you may move a single space while waiting.

Grappling Actions

Wrestling, tackling, pushing opponents around, and other forms of grappling have their own set of actions. Once a grapple has been initiated a roll will determine which opponent has the advantage in the grapple. The opponent in the disadvantaged position can only attempt Escape, Reversal, or Rest actions, and the opponent with the advantage can only use actions from the following list of advantaged grappling actions.

For the duration of the grapple, both opponents occupy the same space. Neither character can attempt to dodge outside attacks, however, neither person is vulnerable to stealth attacks because of this. If a ranged attack targets either character in a grapple roll a single die, on a success the attack hits the intended target, on a failure the attack hits the other character in the grapple.

Grapple
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 4

To start a grapple you must enter the target's space, you can move a single space as part of this action to do so. At the start of the grapple, both opponents roll Strength + Unarmed, the winner of this roll now has the advantage in this grapple and the loser now has the disadvantage.

If attempting to grapple a target that is denied the ability to use defensive actions (such as an unaware or flanked opponent), this targets's initial grapple roll is replaced with a roll is rolled with all automatic successes replaced by dice.

Tackle
Combat Action - Speed Cost: number of spaces crossed

You can also start a grapple by tackling your target. Tackling functions like the Charge action; it can target anyone at least 3 spaces away but within a number of spaces equal to your current speed and accessible via a straight line over even terrain. Tackling someone moves you into their space.

When you tackle, both you and your target make resisted Strength + Unarmed checks. The winner of this roll has the advantaged position and the loser has the disadvantaged position. If either participant won the roll by more than 3 successes, the loser takes a point of damage on a random location.

Advantaged Grappling Actions

Choke
Extended action - Speed Cost: 6

This attack drains the target's endurance instead of doing normal damage. During each full round interval, roll your Strength + Unarmed against a target number equal to your opponent's Stamina rating. Each success above the target number drains one point of Endurance from the target. As soon as you stop this action, or are forced to stop by the target reversing or ending the grapple, the target's Endurance returns to its previous value at the start of the next round.

If the target's Endurance drops to 0 it immediately becomes unconscious. If you continue choking an unconscious target it will lose one point of endurance per round (you no longer have to roll). If the target's endurance reaches the negative of its normal value the target dies. If you stop choking an unconscious opponent their Endurance will return by one point per round until consciousness is regained.

Pin
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 5

Both opponents make resisted Strength + Unarmed rolls, if you win this roll you have gotten your opponent pinned against a floor or wall or have locked them into a submission hold. As long as you remain in the advantaged position you gain a bonus of 1 automatic success to any roll you make as part of a grappling action. Anytime that you have this bonus you cannot take any further Pin actions.

Point Blank Attack
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 4

This action lets you use weapons with the Small trait inside the grapple. You may only attempt this action if you already had the weapon in your hand when the grapple started. You and your target make resisted Strength + Melee checks. If you win the roll the attack hits, but if you lose the roll by more than 3 your opponent has wrestled control of the weapon away from you and may either drop it or hold on to it. If the weapon is a melee weapon roll location and damage normally, if the weapon is a firearm roll location normally and deal maximum damage.

Push
Move Action - Speed Cost: 2 per space moved

This action lets you force the target into another space. Make resisted Strength + Unarmed rolls against your opponent. Each success you beat your opponent by lets you move them a space. Each space moved has a speed cost of 2. If you rolled more successes than you needed to push the target as far as you did, then after pushing you may release your opponent into any adjacent square of your choosing. This allows you to release them into a hazard if you wish.

Release
Free Action - Speed Cost: 1

This ends the grapple, both characters can now act normally. The other character is now in an adjacent space of their choosing.

Rest
Full action - No Speed Cost

Resting functions the same way it does outside of a grapple except that you cannot move. Regain double your normal speed at the start of the next round after using this action.

Throttle
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 4

Roll a location and your Strength score. Each success on the strength roll does one point of damage to the location rolled.

Throw
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 4

This action ends the grapple and moves the target into a different space of your choosing. Roll your Strength score, each success you get lets you throw the target 1 space. For example, if you rolled 3 successes you could move the target into any space within 3 spaces of you.

If the path the target is thrown crosses an occupied space, both the thrown target and the character in the occupied space end up prone in the occupied space and are dealt 1 point of damage to a random location. If the path crosses an easily breakable obstacle (glass, light furniture), the target smashes through it and is dealt 1 point of damage to a random location. If the path crosses an unbreakable object (walls, heavy furniture) the character stops in a space adjacent to the object and is dealt 2 points of damage to a random location. See the Hazards section of chapter 3 to determine the results of throwing an opponent into other hazards (such as fire or large drops).

Disadvantaged Grappling Actions

Escape
Extended Action - Speed Cost: 4

With this action you can eventually escape a grapple, even when the opponent's grappling ability is superior to yours. Make an extended roll of Agility + Unarmed with an interval of a turn. Once this extended roll has accumulated more successes than the sum of your opponent's Strength + Unarmed scores you succeed and exit the grapple into an adjacent space of your choosing and can now act normally.

Reversal
Combat Action - Speed Cost: 4

This action allows you to attempt to gain the upper hand in a grapple. Both characters make resisted Strength + Unarmed rolls, if you win your character is now in the advantaged position.

Rest
Full Action - Speed Cost: 0

Resting functions the same way it does outside of a grapple except that you cannot move. Regain double your normal speed at the start of the next round after using this action.
