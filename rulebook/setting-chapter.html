Setting Basics

Running a game where the characters and plot feel like they exist in a convincingly real and interactive world is more entertaining the alternative. Players shouldn't feel like they are operating in a vacuum populated only by plot devices that keep them constrained to stay on the rails of the storyline you've created. Part of the challenge of keeping a setting feeling authentic is to be ready to improvise unique and realistic locations and characters for your players to interact with. Ideally, the random gas station attendant that you had to make up on the spot when of you players decided to stop and ask for directions should feel as real as the story's main antagonist. You don't need to come up with complex back stories for every stray person your players interact with, but throwing in just a couple unique details for them will keep things interesting.

Some game masters find a lot of satisfaction in creating unique worlds for their story and some simply just tell a story that happens in modern day Everytown, USA. Either approach is fine, it just depends on what story you want to tell and what kind of game your players want to play. The rules in this book assume that your game takes place in modern day or the near future, but that shouldn't stop you from running a space opera, swords & sorcery adventure, or noir detective mystery. To run a game in a different era just restrict or characters to only owning period equipment or make up the stats for any unique equipment that exists in your world.

The rest of this chapter describes the default mythos of the Cascade Effect universe. This setting takes place in Earth's very near future with a few more dangers, conspiracies, adventures, and supernatural beings thrown in for extra flavor. This setting is meant as a starting point for you to work from when creating a storyline, any part of it could be a story side or a plot hook for your characters. Feel free to use any, all, or none of this setting, including the basic information on the nature of Entanglement, styles, and the Yau. In your universe Entanglement could instead be eldritch energy, nano-tech implants, or the manifestations of spirits.

Entanglement & Styles

In the day to day experience of a character, Entanglement is experienced as a resource that can be accumulated and expended to provide the fuel for styles. In a similar light, styles are experienced as the superhuman powers that can be granted by Entanglement. These experiences shed little light on the actual phenomena that allow them to be possible, and as such many people with styles utilize a variety of different terms and conceptual frameworks to attempt to understand and describe them.

At the most fundamental level, Entanglement is just a measure of the intensity and potential bandwidth of the connection between the Entangled person's physical body and a portion of their consciousness residing in the Yau called the Yau body. As styles are activated a portion of the brain to Yau body connection is earmarked for running, maintaining, and recovering from that power. Eventually that portion of the connection will be freed up and available for active use again. This relationship results in the sensation that Entanglement can be used, burned up, or expended until it slowly regenerates.

On the surface, styles are a measure of how experienced and powerful an entangled person is is. The greater the amount of styles a person has, the greater the portion of their mind resides inside their Yau body instead of their brain. This has the effect of giving the person a greater degree of conscious control over the potential powers thier Entanglement can offer. In short, the more self-aware your Yau body is the larger your repertoire of styles can be. Exporting a portion of your consciousness into the Yau is not without side effects, however. Large numbers of styles tend to make people seem less human, less connected to reality, and more prone to experiencing dissociative episodes.

Styles work by increasing the computational capabilities of the Yau body and by allowing the exchange of stored resources and information between the Yau and physical space. Theoretically styles could allow you to directly change any particle or force into anything else, but the process of doing so is extremely difficult to understand. Because of this comprehension difficulty you can only utilize specific styles that you have incrementally gained a deeply intuitive understanding of. It is much easier to enhance the human body's natural attributes and capabilities to superhuman levels than it is to develop. This is why the basic style classes are based on human attributes, and the more difficult to acquire arcane style classes that can directly influence the outside world have attribute based prerequisites.

The Yau body is little more than an external computer that supplements your brain. Styles that improve mental attributes work simply by increasing the size and processing power of the Yau body. They make you seem smarter and wittier by literally making you think faster, retain more information, and store more detailed memories. In addition to improved mental capabilities your Yau body can reach out and gain empathic information about nearby Yau bodies and material information about nearby surroundings to increase attributes like Charisma and Perception.

The way physical styles function is a little more complicated and typically involves benefits from both Yau stored resources and from making actual physiological changes to the body. Attributes like agility and finesse both experience benefits from increased mental processing speed, but also from the ability to directly influence the physical body's position in space by making use of limited control over the fundamental force gravitonertia. Additionally attributes like strength and stamina are both powered by resources stored in the Yau that can be accessed when needed.

Arcane styles primarily function in the most complex way of all, and as such are typically more difficult to acquire due to having prerequisite styles. These styles function by allowing you to directly influence the three fundamental forces, electromagnetism, gravitonertia, and chromodynamism. Theoretically arcane styles could grant you the ability to rewrite any particle or force with anything else, however, the difficulty in comprehending the process of doing so only permits these styles to function in certain primal ways.

Cosmology

For many games cosmology is the last thing the characters or game master have to (or want to) deal with. But stories involving multiverses, time travel, or cosmic psychedelia should ideally have a working model of the universe.

The universe we experience is a bigger place than we can imagine, but it is only a small part of a whole. Every moment of our entire universe is arranged in order as a four-dimensional worldline; at one end is the beginning of time and at the other, the present moment. As we travel down our worldline we can only experience the present moment.

An infinite number of worldlines, every possible worldline, radiate outward from the big bang. Every miniscule decision forges a new branch on its worldline, meaning almost any alternate history you can imagine has happened somewhere. Small parts of the multiverse are stable with worldlines that travel in straight orderly lines causing a local appearance resembling a fractal snowflake. Most of the multiverse is less stable with worldlines that twist, turn, intertwine, jockey for space, and choke each other off. The angle and curve of a worldline influences the values of its physical constants including nature of matter and forces and as well as the speed of light and how fast time passes.

Because of the wildly varying physical laws across worldlines relatively few of them are capable of sustaining solid matter, let alone stars, planets, and intelligent life. In general, the closer together two worldlines are, the more they have in common. However, some twists and turns have brought radically dissimilar worldlines together and some worldlines have even become so intertwined that the boundaries between them have become less clear.

Every worldline is composed of two symmetrical parts; the three-dimensional space we are familiar with and its parallel Yau space. The normal space part of each worldline is discrete and wholly separate from all others. Its contents cannot interact with the contents of other lines. However, the Yau space is less clearly defined and in tightly packed parts of the multiverse it will sometimes blend together with the Yau space of adjacent lines. In such spaces anything capable of entering and traversing the deep Yau could potentially gain access to the adjacent worldlines.

The Yau

The Yau was named as homage to the manifold structures in turn of the century string theory. At every point in normal space there is a tiny three-dimensional toroidal helix shaped manifold. While these manifolds are smaller than matter particles the space inside of them is still enough to give rise to the Yau space phenomenon. Because the manifolds are smaller than matter particles, matter floats on top of the manifold and cannot enter or directly interact with anything inside the Yau.

Some exotic particles are capable of being stable sizes small enough to fit within the Yau manifold. Particles this small naturally sink down into the Yau and exist parallel to, but not influenced by normal material space. This phenomenon results in the manifestation of a second layer of space just below normal material space.

Certain conditions, technologies, or styles are capable contracting an object's (or a living being's) particles down into sizes that allow it to enter the Yau. An object that has entered the Yau in this way is no longer visible, tangible or detectable by any means in normal space.

Yau Interactions

A being in the Yau experiences a sort of shadow realm that mirrors normal space and can move about freely in it. While it can't be detected from normal space, it experiences the shadow of the matter in normal space. Normal objects appear as blurry shadows of their normal selves and for the most part are still solid and cannot be passed through. The less something moves around in normal space the more solid it's Yau shadow is, for example, a building or landform would appear completely solid and opaque in the Yau while a moving car would be little more than a foggy wake of its motion.

The smaller an object's particles get the deeper it can sink into the Yau and the further away from normal space it can get. The deeper something is the less it is influenced by matter in normal space. In general, the depth an object's shadow extends into the Yau is determined by how often it moves. In the very shallow Yau everything is completely solid. A little deeper in the Yau objects that are in constant motion are transparent enough that they can be passed through with ease, traffic and crowds no longer present any obstacles but walls and doors do. It is extremely difficult for a concious being in the Yau to go deeper than this level but if they do things become even more transparent, at medium depth doors and furniture give out, deeper and some newer buildings are gone too, deeper still and nothing is left but the earth. In the extremely deep Yau the landscape itself is nothing more than a blurry shade that can be easily moved through.

The laws of physics also change the deeper you go into the Yau. One consequence of this is that the deeper a being travels in the Yau the more difficult it is to maintain meaningful levels of conciousness. Going too deep could even permanently damage or destroy your pysche. In the very deep Yau even gravity and distance start to break down. A being in the extremely deep Yau you could potentially float around at the equivalent of super-luminal speeds effortlessly, however, due to the extreme difficulty associated with getting to this depth and maintaining conciousness there this phenomenon rarely ever experienced in a meaningful way.

A being in the Yau is incapable of interacting with an object in or anchored to physical space. This includes the shadows of living things and the objects that have been placed into the Yau by styles or technology. People that have used the style Sublimation to bring their entire body into the Yau can, however, interact with each other and any objects they have on their person as long as they stay at similar depths. Most rolls and rules, including those pertaining to combat, would function as normal between completely sublimated beings.

Sound and light do not propagate the same way they do in normal space rendering communication between beings in the Yau is very difficult. Sound does not exist at all, so speech is impossible and most beings in the Yau are too blurry to make sign language a functional alternative. A being can make short lived marks by brushing against an object's shadow, so slow communication via large scale writing is possible between beings that share a written language. Two Yau bodies can also transfer information between themselves as long as they have similar levels of conciousness and remain directly touching one another. This process conveys concepts much more slowly than speaking, a rate of about a sentence per minute is typical. Styles and technologies that allow telethesia function normally in the Yau and even between the Yau and physical space.

Yau Biology

Over the course of Earth's history life gradually evolved the ability to utilize Yau scale interactions as part of the biological process. While it can't interact directly with the Yau, the nervous system of most vertebrates is nonetheless capable of indirectly influencing the spin of adjacent particles in the shallow Yau. This capability allows the organism to utilize the particles in its Yau shadow as a sort of external computation organ called the Yau body that acts in parallel to the brain. The neurochemical computer that is the brain is responsible for most things we recognize as thinking but for some computationally intensive activities it is supplemented by the particle spin computer of its Yau body. Some higher mammals also have evolved the ability to not just use Yau computation but to actively attract more particles to their Yau shadow to increase its density and computational power.

The Yau wake of a human is a dense and powerful processor that the brain utilizes to supplement almost every thought process. However, an organism's Yau body cannot typically store information, because of this your long term memory, personality, and self-awareness, everything that makes you you, all reside almost entirely in your physical brain. Without a connection to the brain your Yau body is just a processor, it is not a mind.

In most cases when the body dies its Yau body dissipates into nothingness shortly afterwards. If the Yau body is somehow severed from its host brain the host will be rendered unconscious for about an hour. During this period the original Yau body will dissipate and the brain will recreate its original particle spin connections, effectively growing a new Yau body. After about an hour the body regains consciousness but is unable to think clearly and has its Intelligence and Wits attributes both reduced to 0. These attributes will be restored to their original values at a rate of a point every hour. A person with Entanglement has a more robust Yau body is more resistant to effects that would normally sever a Yau body and only loses a point of Entanglement instead of falling unconscious and having drained attributes. However, an extremely powerful event could potentially still remove all of a person's Entanglement at once and sever the Yau body completely.

The Yau & Entanglement

Styles exist because of Yau scale interactions. The difference between a normal human and a person with styles is that the the styled person has exported a portion of their consciousness to their Yau body. This allows them a degree of conscious control over their Yau body's functions and the ability for it to remain somewhat self-aware even if the connection to the brain is severed. The amount of Entanglement a person has is equal to the number of connections the person has to their Yau body. Each connection increases the potential bandwidth between the brain and the Yau body. Using Entanglement occupies a portion of the connections with maintaining that power and then recovering from its use over time. This effectively renders that portion unusable until it has recovered. Because of this people experience these connections as a resource, a source of energy that can be used or depleted and regenerates over time.

Some people with styles have taken to refering to Entanglement as Crisis. This name was given because people can sometimes experience sudden gains in the amount of Entanglement they have in high tension, risky, dynamic, or fast moving situations. This phenomenon happens because fast moving or high potential objects cause the near Yau to become blurry and less defined and the greater the state of flux the Yau is in the easier it is for a Yau body to create connections. Additionally, lots of motion in physical space can cause minor cascade events that add a directional pressure current in the Yau that biases information towards either entering or leaving the Yau, which also can reduce or increase the difficulty of forging new Entanglement connections. When an area with static, unmoving, and heavily defined Yau structures suddenly bursts into action the Yau body subconsciously takes advantage of the new found ease of forging connections and will attempt to make them which results in gaining a point of Entanglement.

Association

For a normal person the Yau body is typically an just an unconscious calculator and a long term memory storage, but for a a person with Entanglement the increased connectivity can result in the Yau Body becoming more than that. The greater the number of connections a person has the greater the amount of their consciousness and self-awareness their Yau body processes. One of the consequences of having a consciousness divided between two organs that are not always consistently connected is varying types of dissociative episodes.

At one point or another almost everyone with Entanglement experiences some level of dissociation from having a current Entanglement total that is far apart from their normal Entanglement score. A very low total results in a surreal and depressing experience depersonalization and of the feeling of being an external witness to one's own body. Alternatively, a level well above the limit results in anxiety, mania, and a feeling of derealization where the world no longer seems real or that you are operating somehow outside of reality.

While alarming, typical dissociative episodes are only the beginning of some of the strange things that can happen as a result of a self-aware Yau body. People with Entanglement that have not yet become familiar with it enough to have styles sometimes experience hypnogogic out of body experiences because they truly do have a certain amount of consciousness residing outside of their body. In rare circumstances, certain combinations of highly conscious Yau bodies and high levels of dissociation can result in a severed Yau body becoming a separate entity with its self-awareness and agency. This is most likely to happen at a death, near death, or coma situation that causes the body to shed the Yau body.

Dissociation

Once separated from its original mind a Yau body entity, or Yau spirit, can move freely about the Yau. The amount of awareness and agency that a severed Yau spirit has varies greatly. Some are just simple animalistic repeating loops of instinct lost in the seemingly alien Yauscape while others are practically complete copies of the original person's personality that retain varying amounts of their original memories.

A free moving Yau spirits is most commonly created when its original body dies, however, most deaths do not result in a Yau spirit; they are only created when a person dies during a highly dissociative environment such as a cascade event. It's also possible, but rare, for powerful dissociative events such as a near death experience, certain future technologies, or an extremely massive sudden Entanglement gain or loss to cause a Yau body to separate and leave the original body temporarily weakened but otherwise alive and ready to regenerate a new Yau body over time.

Experiencing a complete dissociation event of this magnitude typically has serious mental side effects, especially if the severed Yau body contained within it a substantial portion of its owner's personality. In general, the greater the amount of Entanglement a person has the more resistant they are to a dissociative event and the more mental side effects they suffer should this resistance fail.

A few hours after total dissociation a person's conciousness, Wits, and Intelligence will have returned like anyone else's would have but the mental side effects may linger on. These effects most commonly include personality changes (these can be minor or extreme, and frequently involve the developments resembling Axis II personality disorders), memory loss (anywhere from a short blackout to total retrograde amnesia), or in extreme cases temporarily diminished skills and mental attributes (the traits most often decreased are intelligence, wits, or any skill that has been improved by styles). These mental effects are usually temporary but may last weeks or months as the person acclimates to a new Yau body.

It is possible for a severed Yau spirit to reassociate with its original host body. If this happens any mental side effects the host body was suffering will typically be reversed over the course of the next few hours. The likelihood of a reunion happening increases in proportion to the amount of awareness imparted into the Yau body. Assuming it wants to, a spirit with enough agency can simply follow its original body until the association becomes possible (this is usually after the body has completely recovered from the dissociation event and is most easily accomplished during a period of diminished consciousness such as sleep).

Yau spirits are relatively common in the Yau but most of them are barely conscious. These tend to float aimlessly from place to place or follow repeating behavioral loops. Conscious spirits are fairly rare and not always willing to communicate. The degree of awareness in a spirit slowly fades over the course of years until they are reduced to almost nothing.

Cascade Events

The same types of events that can trigger dissociation events in humans, if powerful enough, can trigger cascade events in locations. Massive expenditures of Entanglement will briefly change the way the Yau behaves at the affected location. Sometimes this can trigger a chain reaction that can cause these changes to be self-sustaining and persist for varying lengths of time. These persisting changes to the nature of the Yau are referred to as cascade events.

Depending on what triggers them, cascade events vary in duration, intensity, size, and type of effects. While no cascade event is truly permanent they, can last for any amount of time from a few seconds to tens of thousands of years. They usually only affect a small area, a single room, a house, or at most a small neighborhood. All cascade events are highly dissociative; spending extended periods of time in them increase the likelihood experiencing dissociative episodes. A person spending a substantial portion of their youth inside certain kinds of cascade events might come away from the experience more likely to develop greater levels of Entanglement than usual. The primary types of cascade events are positive polarity events, negative polarity events, genius locii events, incursion events, non-locality events, and total wave function collapses. The duration and locations of cascade events are entirely up to the game master.

Positive Polarity Event

These events reduce the difficulty for things to exit the Yau into normal space and increase the difficulty to enter the Yau. Positive polarity cascades are usually created by something massive exiting the Yau or an extremely large and rapid expenditure of Entanglement. These events are typically intense but short lived. Being inside an ongoing positive polarity area has the following effects.

  • Daily Entanglement balancing always increases your current Entanglement total, even if it is already above your normal Entanglement score.
  • Using any style within the Sublimation class to enter the Yau is more difficult and now requires you to succeed on a roll of Willpower + Guts. The difficulty of this roll depends on the intensity of the cascade event and is up to the GM. You can still use Sublimation to exit the Yau as normal.
  • Any style or technology that stores items in the Yau cannot be used. Items already stored in the Yau can still be retrieved normally.
  • Depending on the intensity of the area, items stored in the Yau can become visible in normal space. Varying levels of intensity will cause stored items to fluctuate through levels of transparency as you move about through the area. The visual effects of this may alarm bystanders as you pass through the area, especially if the stored items being revealed are weapons or otherwise threatening.
  • In rare and extreme events it may be possible for some stronger Yau spirits to manifest visibly in the physical space of these areas. These manifestations can be alarming but are rarely more than Yau spirits becoming slightly visible or creating phantom sounds. They typically do not have the power to move or influence physical objects.
  • Positive polarity events are not as dissociative as most other cascade events, so other than the possible visual manifestations they are difficult for normal people to notice. Long periods of time spent in these events will grant the advantage of increased Entanglement resources, but at the cost of the extreme levels of anxiety and frequent Crisis sickness that come with that.
Negative Polarity Event

The opposite of positive polarity events, negative events make it easier to enter the Yau but more difficult to exit it. These chain reactions are usually triggered by ambient accumulations of Entangled elements in the environment gathering over time or something large slowly slipping into the Yau. Because of the likely causes of these events they are usually fairly weak but long lasting. Being inside an ongoing negative polarity event has the following effects.

  • Daily Entanglement balancing always decreases your current Entanglement total, even if it is already below your normal Entanglement score.
  • It is easier than normal to enter the Yau using any Sublimation style, as such there is no longer a Entanglement cost to do so. Exiting the Yau, however, is more difficult and requires you to succeed on a roll of Willpower + Guts. The difficulty of this roll depends on the intensity of the cascade event and is up to the GM.
  • Items stored in the Yau by styles or technology cannot be retrieved. Additional items can still be stored, but nothing can be retrieved until you have exited the area.
  • The bottleneck these events place on information traveling from the Yau body to the brain results in a greater degree of information being held in the Yau body than usual, because of this even minor negative polarity events are intensely dissociative, especially to people with many styles. The dissociative experience coupled with the fatigue caused by the slow Entanglement drain makes these areas unpleasant for Entangled people to be in for extended periods. However, in normal people, long term exposure to the dissociation caused by these events can have the effect of increasing the likelihood of developing increased Entanglement levels or even styles.
Genius Locii Event

Genius locii can be created by events that have somehow created, destroyed, or merged Yau spirits. These events involve multiple Yau spirits merging into a single collective semi-consciousness. While they are usually less self-aware than before, these merged consciousnesses do exhibit more influence over physical space. Genius locii are tied to the area in which the event occurred that created them. The greater ability to affect normal space comes at the expense of being able to move around.

  • These events bear a superficial resemblance many classic literary haunting phenomena. Temperature fluctuations, electromagnetic disturbances, inexplicable feelings of dread or unease, and phantom noises are all capable of being caused by Genius locii. One of these events paired with a positive polarity event can even induce visible apparitions.
  • Genius locii can create poltergeist activity. Minor examples include objects moving when no one is looking or closing doors. More intense versions will violently throw objects around a room, potentially causing injuries.
  • Genius locii influence normal space through the same cascade effects that power a person's styles. As such, it is potentially possible for extremely powerful and self-aware genius locii to create any effect that could be created by a style. However, due to the fact that most of these spirits are barely self-aware, shattered, confused, and disoriented they rarely exhibit anything greater than the typical ghost like behavior.
  • Because they are an amalgamation of several partial consciousnesses, these spirits usually behave in a very confused seemingly random fashion. Only rarely do they come to enough of a consensus to act in any sort of goal oriented fashion. On the occasion that they do the goal is rarely anything more than attempting to communicate or understand their situation, however, it is possible for genius locii to be actively hostile or to attempt to further the goals of one of the more self-aware spirits that went into creating it.
Total Wavefunction Collapse

Total wavefunction collapse events result in the removal of probability and access to the Yau in the affected area. Along with nonlocality events, these events are among the most intense of all possible cascade events and are only created by the most extreme circumstances. Being inside an ongoing total wavefunction collapse has the following effects.

  • Nothing can enter or exit the Yau under any circumstances.
  • You are considered to have 0 Entanglement. Upon exiting the area your Entanglement reverts to its previous value.
  • Styles no longer function. As long as you remain in this area you are considered to have no ranks in any style. Improved skills and attributes revert to their natural values. Upon leaving the area your styles and traits revert to their normal values.
  • Regardless of what depth you are in inside the Yau all shadows of physical objects are completely hard edged and opaque, nothing can pass through anything else in the Yau.
  • Probability and random chance do not exist. A normally random event will have the same outcome every time. When attempting an action that requires a roll, instead give yourself a number of automatic successes equal to half (rounding down) of the number of dice you would normally roll.
  • Spending an extended period of time in a total wavefunction collapse inevitably results in completely severing your Yau body. The speed at which this happens depends on the intensity of the event. This effect eventually happens to everyone if they remain in the area, but it happens more slowly to people with Entanglement. Once this happens to a person they fall unconscious until they are either removed from the area or the event ends.
Nonlocality Event

Nonlocality events are extremely intense events that are the opposite of total wavefunction collapses. In a nonlocality event the effects of probability are heightened and the Yau and physical spaces blend together. Being inside an ongoing nonlocality event has the following effects.

  • Due to the extreme ease of accessing the Yau, anyone with any ranks in Sublimation is considered to have every rank of every Sublimation style.
  • Every object's Yau shadow is blurry, transparent, and can be passed through at any depth of the Yau.
  • All styles can be activated without an Entanglement cost.
  • All items stored in the Yau become completely visible in normal space and can be accessed and interacted with by anyone that is close enough to you to touch them.
  • The effects of probability and randomness are exacerbated. When attempting an action that requires a roll convert all automatic successes to an equal number of dice.
  • Anyone with styles can gain additional temporary style points to be used as you see fit when spending extended periods of time inside nonlocality events. The rate at which these points accrue is determined by the intensity of the event and is up to the GM. Upon leaving the event the styles purchased with these extra points go away. If you return to the same event while it is still ongoing the styles you purchased may also return.

Avatar Creation

Under certain circumstances it can become possible for Yau spirits to inhabit physical bodies. This can happen as the result of the spirit either taking possession of a living body or by the spirit building an entirely new body out of raw materials gathered from the environment. Possession or avatar creation events are rare and are typically an emergent phenomenon of the random acts of barely conscious spirits and long term cascade events.

A living body can only be possessed if the consciousness residing in its brain is in some way compromised, for example, by a coma, brain injury, or drug induced state. A spirit succeeding in possessing a body with no self-awareness remaining will now have a permanent physical body. A very powerful spirit could even attempt to possess a sleeping body, however, any body that still possesses a conscious mind can roll Willpower + Guts as an extended action to eventually evict the spirit. The number of successes required increases with the strength and amount of consciousness in the Yau spirit but any conscious mind will eventually succeed in ridding itself of the possessor.

The personality created by a possession is not a pure expression of the possessing spirit but rather an amalgamation of that of the host body and the possessor with the mental attributes of the physical body. The greater the amount self-awareness in the possessing spirit the more the resulting possessed body's personality will resemble it. A possession is more likely to occur inside a genius locii or positive polarity event. Possessions resulting from genius locii tend to create particularly unstable personalities as different aspects of the merged spirits vie against the original body for control.

Another way for a Yau spirit to get a physical body is to create an avatar. In order for this to happen the spirit must find a suitable undisturbed location that currently is experiencing greater Yau influence than normal such as during a positive polarity or nonlocality event. This location must also have adequate physical resources to construct a body out of. While it is possible for a body to be built purely out of atoms caught from the air, it is much easier, quicker, and more likely to succeed if the location has large quantities of liquid water and organic molecules. Ideal locations include stagnant water, muddy pits, and Elusain wet rooms. Building an avatar typically takes weeks, but a particularly rich vein of resources coupled with a very intense cascade event and a very determined spirit can get the job done in just a couple of days. Conversely, a barren location, weak cascade event, or barely aware spirit could take months or even years to accrete a functional body.

If at any point during the process the avatar creation site is disturbed or moved the process fails and must begin anew, however, the second attempt may be able to recycle some of the previous materials and expedite the process. If someone discovers the avatar creation site they will find what appears to be a decaying dead body with no life signs. If this body is witnessed over enough time while left undisturbed in situ the apparent decay will seem to reverse as the body develops into a functioning avatar. When the avatar awakens it will act as a permanent new body for the Yau spirit. In most cases the avatar has the same physical appearance, skills, and attributes as the spirit's original body, however, if the spirit does not remember the original body or never had one then it will have the stats of a newly created character. Awakening in a new avatar is always a disorienting experience, whatever memories the spirit possessed may be incomplete, inaccurate, or missing entirely.

While most possessions and avatar creations happen as a result of the random actions and desires of barely conscious spirits, they can also be done by the highly conscious spirit of a person with styles. A very heavily styled person, in particular a someone with styles from the arcane class Sublimation that has learned to navigate the Yau well, could potentially learn to attempt possession or avatar creation on demand. The more Sublimation ranks a person has the better they are at possessions, building avatars quickly, and retaining memories after death and resurrection. Someone that has become adept enough at this to respond to death by simply getting a new body is effectively immortal and could only be destroyed by completely destroying their Yau body before a physical body can be created. These capabilities are represented by the three extension styles, Avatar, Permanence, and Proxy.

After repeated avatar creations, a person's physical body may no longer internally resemble a normal human body as it instead operates primarily inside the Yau and maintains only the barest minimum of matter required to interact with physical space, an avatar manifesting this quality may weigh less than half of what a normal body of its shape, size, and strength normally would.

Worldline Incursions

Similar to how cascade events, avatars, and possessions allow the immaterial to reach out from the Yau and influence physical space, worldline incursion events allow for things completely alien to our universe to enter our world. Worldline incursions can occur when two multiversal timelines become entangled and pushed together tightly enough that each worldline's Yau space becomes mixed together. When this happens it becomes possible for an object in the Yau of one worldline to enter the Yau of the other one, if this object is capable of exiting the Yau and can then do so in the other world. During an incursion only the deepest part of the Yau becomes enmeshed with the neighboring worldline and as such the incursion can only be detected or crossed by the most determined of Yau travelers or extremely advanced technology.

Worldline incursions actually happen frequently, but most of them go unnoticed because they are just incursions with nearby worldlines that are so similar that they are practically identical, however, it is possible for more distant, and therefore more different, worldlines to be brought together. Incursions can last any amount of time from mere minutes to going unnoticed for centuries. They can be isolated single events or ongoing cycles that cause incursions that last a certain amount of time every time a certain number of years have passed. Incursions can also vary in intensity; greater intensity events become more detectable because they allow things to cross from one worldline to the other at increasingly shallow depths in the Yau.

Most incursions result in contact with an alternate worldline Earth. While most of the time the two Earths are almost identical it is possible to connect two Earths at different points in history, two earths with different histories, or even two Earths with different evolutionary history populated by entirely different life. While possible, worldline incursions that result in the contact with more distant worldlines are exceedingly rare. Many variables need to be just right in order for this to happen. A distant worldline may have different physics that don't allow for stars, planets, and life. While physically similar worldlines are attracted to one another, the odds of connecting with one that has a habitable planet near your location are small.

Artifacts & The Simulation

At some point in the past century a massive worldline incursion resulted in a large number of artifacts from a distant worldline being deposited around the globe. While these rare artifacts include everything from cultural objects and personal effects to advanced technology, but the most commonly represented items are small pieces of advanced military hardware including small arms, personal armor, and computer components. Characters can acquire these arms and equipment to augment their capabilities, but perhaps the largest impact on our world is caused by the computing hardware.

Over time a significant amount of the extra-worldline computing hardware been gathered by a clearly intentional and intelligent being, or group of beings, to establish a global network. This network is housed in several strategically placed server clusters assembled out of improvised combinations of mundane computing hardware and extra-worldline components. The network broadcasts a global signal on a wavelength that is shorter than a Yau manifold and is thus undetectable by mundane means. Running on the server network is a highly realistic simulation of the entire planet that certain pieces of artifact technology capable of communicating on the correct wavelength can connect to. Artifact technology that can connect to the simulation and contains an appropriate brain-computer interface can send its user's consciousness into the simulation to interact with it and with any other consciousnesses that are also connected to it.

The simulation contains an accurate recreation of the of the topography of Earth that operates on a highly realistic simulation of the basic laws of physics and motion. While the simulation is a highly accurate mirror of the landforms of Earth, the specific details of locales vary wildly in accuracy, detail, and resolution. It would appear that details on the scale of vegetation and man made structures have been rendered manually in the simulation and as such some areas are highly detailed and some areas have been glossed over. The most accurate areas are almost indistinguishable from reality while other areas are a loose approximation rendered in low-resolution voxels. Some areas have even been completely ignored and are simple barren unvegetated landscapes. There are also areas that have been rendered in high levels of detail but do not correspond to reality at all, such as a structure or a city that is a complete fabrication with no real world analogue. It is clear that this simulation has been heavily used, modified and updated over the years but at the present it seems mostly abandoned. Most areas are out of date to some degree and have structures as the were years or decades ago.

elusain

The Elusain are a small population of beings that ended up on Earth as the result of a massive worldline incursion. This incursion happened repeatedly with varying levels of intensity over the course of the 1900s and reached its peak in the late 60's. This incursion is also the source of a substantial portion of artifact technology, most which appears to be Elusain in origin.

The Elusain are a humanoid species with an evolutionary heritage primarily consisting a group of semi-aquatic, semi-arboreal creatures well adapted to life in the oxygen poor, nutrient rich densely forested swamp lands common in their native worldline's version of Earth. Elusain have smooth hairless skin that varies in color from light tans and yellows to dark greenish browns. Some have darker dorsal and lighter ventral coloration patterns as a vestige of their aquatic heritage. They have long slim bodies supported a flexible lordosis curved spine and are typically 6 to 7 feet in height. Their long streamlined limbs terminate in highly dextrous hands and feet well adapted for grasping, climbing, and swimming with four long slender semi-webbed digits opposed by two thmublike digits. The Elusain have rather minimal facial features, dominated by a pair of large, dark, lidless eyes protected with a nictating membrane. They have no mouth or nose, but rather a large triangular nasal slit protected and partially hidden by folds of skin.

Elusain physiology is well adapted for, but also heavily dependant on water. While they do not posess gills and must breath air they can stay comfortably submerged in oxygenated water for up to an hour by absorbing small amounts of oxygen transdermally. Elusain must completely submerge themselves frequently.
