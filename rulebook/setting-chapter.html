This section describes the world of Cascade Effect as the average person in that world would understand it. All new players are encouraged to read this section.

The following sections marked as CLASSIFIED describe the secret workings of the world that most people, including most starting characters, would not know about. As such, all sections marked CLASSIFIED should not be read without permission from your GM.

Things Most People Know

Cascade Effect takes place in a world that is much like our own, but it is troubled with more drama, conflict, and risk of danger. In this world mysteries are more likely to remain unsolved, conspiracies are more likely to be real, violence is more likely to occur, and crimes more likely to go ignored. The average person isn't as sure that the strange rumor they heard or that creepy video they saw were just hoaxes. One thing is certain though, every mystery has a perfectly rational scientific explanation, but only if you can find it.

It's the very near future, just a few years ahead of the real world. In the intervening years , technology hasn't had time to advance much but the world has gotten more polarized. Politics have become strictly black and white, inequality has gotten much worse, and these conflicts are only accelerating. As tensions rise, many rebellious groups from every part of the political and social spectrum have risen up. Massive protests have become commonplace, and some factions are even talking about secession or revolution. The threat of civil wars and violent revolutions loom on the horizon and perceptions of societal precarity practically ubiquitous.

On Polarity

At the turn of the century, the democratization and diffusion of access to the means to create mass media was expected to bring about a new age of transparency and a blossoming of journalism and the arts. Unfortunately, two decades later it has had almost the exact opposite effect. The unintended consequence of the long tail of society claiming the reigns of the media is that all media outlets have had to become increasingly partisan in order to stay competitive. Because one can simply create the evidence needed to support one's views, the most radical voices have become the loudest voices.

Content is now so biased that it has become extremely difficult for even the most informed and educated people to tell fact from fiction. Disinformation, conspiracy theories, pseudoscience, and hoaxes run rampant. This issue is compounded by the fact that in the world of Cascade Effect, these ideas are much more likely to be to reflect reality.

Recent World Events

Global politics have become defined by the conflict between xenophobic nationalists and overoptimistic revolutionaries. The voice of the moderate liberal or conservative is practically unheard.

The Eurozone is being torn apart by disagreements over austerity measures and social safety nets. The recent rise in intense nationalism and xenophobia has been felt most acutely in the European Union as the list of countries considering exciting it grows longer.

The Arab world is erupting into a second Arab Spring. The #NSS movement is making sudden and historically surprising progress in its goal to separate Western Sahara from the kingdom of Morocco and rebuild it as New Secular Sahara, the first nation to be entirely solar and desalinization fueled. Conversely, the Middle East is still in the process of trying to overthrow despots and elected leaders alike in the conflict between western ideals and religious fundamentalism.

Meanwhile, Russia is collapsing under the weight of sanctions, increasingly active civil rights movements, and increased military expenditures. South America and Sub-Saharan Africa both have been witness to uprisings and civil wars that some believe may have been proxy wars hinting at the start of a new cold war.

ID Programs

Citing fears of foreign actors and domestic terrorism, several nations, including the United States, Great Britain, France, China, and Japan, have enacted long-range radio-frequency ID programs. The specifics of these programs vary by country, but in general they work by requiring all citizens to carry radio frequency identification cards at all times. These ID cards can be quickly scanned by a handheld device from a distance of up to 20 meters, allowing law enforcement officials to easily know if someone is carrying their ID, who they are, a list of recent locations that ID has been scanned at, and any information available in federal databases about that person.

The passage and enforcement of each of these programs have proven to be very difficult, particularly in the United States and China where they have been met with widespread dissent and protest. Each ID program has faced both immense political opposition, often including violent protest, and large economic challenges. Due to these difficulties, mandatory ID laws only just starting to be enforced in the largest cities, turning them into panopticons of surveillance. Many people that originally opposed and protested the programs, especially those with strong leftist or libertarian views have moved to more rural areas to avoid this.

Organizations

In light of all of the recent polarization in the world, many new organizations and movements have been created, here are a couple example groups.

The New Frontier

The growing panopticon of large cities coupled with the dropping cost of cheap solar panels has created the Frontier movement, a blanket term for groups that set off to the countryside to create new settlements and new ways to live.

The movement is named after the first group to do this, The New Frontier, A leftist anarcho-socialist group.

There are three New Frontier settlements across the American West. Each of which looks more like a cross between Burning Man and a ramshackle solar plant than a traditional city.

Every person contributing to a settlement is provided for to the best of the rest of the settlement's ability in an ad-hoc fashion.

The Wolf Tribe

Another Frontier group, the Wolf Tribe is a string of settlements populated Libertarian survivalists.

Each settlement is entirely financed by its wealthy founder's stockpile of anything from fiat currency to gold, oil, or ammunition.

Advocates for overthrowing the government by any means, including violent revolution.

Arrondissement Fantôme
(Phantom District)

An organization European of accelerationists that believes that the fastest way to change society for the better is to tear down the current system and start from scratch. They work to get extremists into power in the hope that it will bring about a violent revolution more quickly.

Many people have heard of this group and its primary goal, but it also has two secret subdivisions, each acting to hasten a different revolution.

The Simulationists want to bring about the technological singularity.

Another subdivision wants to establish an interplanetary and/or extra-worldline society. This subdivision is known to actively work with the extra-worldline organization Ceux Sans Cœur, and may actually be little more than a front for this group.

Classified Information

OCTET CYAN

The following information is classified OCTET CYAN. If you do not have OCTET CYAN security clearance discontinue reading immediately.

Documents classified OCTET CYAN contain information pertaining to the existence of individuals that are capable of utilizing the Cascade Effect, and organizations thereof.

Executive Abstract

  • Individuals that are capable of utilizing a controlled cascade effect to gain abilities that appear supernatural exist.
  • These abilities, referred to as styles, are powered by Entanglement, a measure of how connected a person is to the Yau.
  • Normal people can be disabled by severing their connection to the Yau, but people with Styles instead only lose a portion of their Entanglement.
  • Numerous organizations of people with styles exist. The details of these groups, their goals, and dossiers of their leaders are presented at the end of this section.

Entangled Individuals

Throughout the world, people with the ability to create a self-sustaining cascade effect in order to manifest seemingly superhuman abilities exist. These abilities are referred to as Styles, and the people that are able to learn them do so by causing a cascade of information to travel between their body and a spatial phenomenon known as the Yau. The degree to which a person is connected to the Yau is referred to as Entanglement. Further details on the nature of the Yau are classified CONTEXT SHANNON and not in the purview of this section.

People with a heightened connection to the Yau have always existed. In fact, it's plausible that many historical figures that are viewed as extremely competent may have had some measure of Entanglement or styles. It is difficult to measure how prevalent these individuals currently are, but estimates predict that they make up approximately 0.01% of the global population. There is also evidence that this rate has varied greatly throughout history and has risen significantly in recent years.

There does not appear to be any demographic or geographic pattern to the emergence of entangled people. However, exposure to cascade events, dissociative episodes, and individuals with Styles all correlate with new people becoming entangled.

Degrees of Entanglement

The level to which an entangled person can manifest abilities varies greatly. People can be sorted by their degree of capability into three increasingly powerful and increasingly rare categories; The Entangled, hyper-competents, and The Styled.

The Entangled are the most common group. They have a small measure of Entanglement but no knowledge or ability to do anything with it. These people do not have any Styles and likely will never learn any, but their increased Entanglement makes them more resistant to cascade events and being disconnected from the Yau. Some of the Entangled are capable of using the basic Entanglement capabilities of Technique and Recover, however they are typically not aware of this ability and instead just feel like they can focus extra hard on a task to excel at it.

Hyper-competent people are slightly less common than the Entangled. These people have a few styles, but only styles that improve their attributes and skills somewhat. Most of them are incapable of improving beyond the normal limit imposed by the human body, and thus they still only have attributes and skills with a maximum of 3 points and they cannot learn any specialty or auxiliary styles. These people do not appear to have any superhuman abilities, they just appear to be very competent in many fields and extremely successful in their area of expertise.

Of all the people with a connection to the Yau, The Styled are the least common but the most capable. They have true mastery over their Entanglement and can learn any style.

Experiencing Entanglement

People with mastery over their Entanglement is experience it as a resource that can be accumulated and expended to fuel for styles, and styles are experienced as the superhuman powers that can be granted by Entanglement. These experiences shed little light on the actual phenomena that allow them to be possible, and as such many people with styles utilize a variety of different terms and conceptual frameworks to attempt to understand and describe them.

At the most fundamental level, Entanglement is just a measure of the intensity and potential bandwidth of the connection between a person's physical body and the portion of their consciousness that resides in the Yau called the Yau body. As styles are activated a portion of this connection is earmarked for running, maintaining, and recovering from that style. Eventually that portion of the connection will be freed up and available for active use again. This relationship results in the sensation that Entanglement can be used, burned up, or expended until it slowly regenerates.

Entanglement Acquisition

Normally, people with Entanglement have a point at which their Entanglement is in balance. They will slowly gain or lose Entanglement over time until they are at this point. However, certain circumstances may greatly accelerate this process.

Before Orthodynamic Field Theory postulated an explanation for what Entanglement is, many people with styles referred to Entanglement as Crisis. This name was given because people can sometimes experience sudden gains in the amount of Entanglement they have in high tension, risky, dynamic, or fast moving situations.

This phenomenon happens because fast moving objects and objects with high potential energy cause the shallow Yau to become blurry and less defined. The greater the state of flux in the nearby Yau, the easier it is for a Yau body to create connections. When an area with static, unmoving, and heavily defined Yau structures suddenly bursts into action the Yau body subconsciously takes advantage of the new found ease of forging connections and will attempt to make them which results in gaining a point of Entanglement.

Properties of Styles

On the surface, styles are a measure of how experienced a person is at using their Entanglement. The greater the number of styles a person has, the greater the portion of their mind resides inside their Yau body instead of their brain. This has the effect of giving the person a greater degree of conscious control over the potential powers their Entanglement can offer. The more self-aware your Yau body is the larger your repertoire of styles can be. Exporting a portion of your consciousness into the Yau is not without side effects, however. Large numbers of styles tend to make people seem less human, less connected to reality, and more prone to experiencing dissociative episodes.

Styles work by increasing the computational capabilities of the Yau body and by allowing the exchange of stored resources and information between the Yau and physical space. Theoretically, styles could allow you to directly change any particle or force into anything else, but the process of doing something that precise is extremely difficult to comprehend. Because of the challenge of comprehension people can only utilize specific styles that they have incrementally gained a deeply intuitive understanding of. It is much easier to enhance the human body's natural attributes and capabilities to greater levels than it is to directly influence reality. This is why the basic style classes are based on human attributes, and the more difficult to acquire arcane style classes that can directly influence the outside world have attribute based prerequisites.

The Yau body is little more than an external computer that supplements the brain. Styles that improve mental attributes work simply by increasing the size and processing power of the Yau body. They make a person seem smarter and wittier by literally making them think faster, retain more information, and store more detailed memories. In addition to improved mental capabilities, a Yau body can reach out and gain empathic information about nearby Yau bodies and material information about nearby surroundings to increase attributes like Charisma and Perception.

Information entering physical space from the Yau also has the side effect of locally reversing entropy. This anti-entropic property is powerful enough to perform thermodynamic work, allowing people with styles to improve physical attributes. Attributes like Agility and Finesse both experience benefits from increased mental processing speed, but also from using the anti-entropy cascade to manipulate the position of the body. Attributes like Strength and Stamina can be greatly enhanced by this process and also by storing biological resources in the Yau, such as red blood cells, oxygen, or extra muscle mass, and accessing them on demand.

Arcane styles function in the most complex way of all, and as such are typically more difficult to acquire due to having prerequisite styles. These styles function by allowing you to directly influence the three fundamental forces, electromagnetism, gravitonertia, and chromodynamism. Theoretically, arcane styles could grant you the ability to rewrite any particle or force with anything else, however, the difficulty in comprehending the process of doing so only permits these styles to function in certain intuitively primal ways.

Dissociation

For a normal person, the Yau body is typically an just an unconscious calculator and a long-term memory storage, but for a person with Entanglement the increased connectivity can result in the Yau Body becoming more than that. The greater the number of connections a person has, the greater the portion of consciousness and self-awareness their Yau body processes. One of the consequences of having a consciousness divided between two organs that are not always consistently connected is varying types of dissociative episodes.

A common trigger of a dissociative episode is cascade events. These events add a directional pressure to information traveling either from or to the Yau. This pressure can trigger dissociative experiences in anyone. Some events are even powerful enough to sever someone's link to the Yau entirely. If this happens to a normal person, they will immediately fall unconscious and remain so until their connection has recovered. If this happens to a person with Entanglement, they will instead lose some of their Entganglement and have a dissociative episode and remain conscious.

At one point or another, almost everyone with Entanglement experiences some level of dissociation. This most frequently happens when the current Entanglement total is well above or well below the person's normal Entanglement maximum. A very low total will result in a surreal and depressing experience of depersonalization and of the feeling of being an external witness to one's own body. Alternatively, Entanglement levels well above the limit result in anxiety, mania, and a feeling of derealization where the world no longer seems real or that you are no longer a part of the real world.

While alarming, typical dissociative episodes are only the beginning of some of the strange things that can happen as a result of a self-aware Yau body. People with Entanglement that have not yet become familiar enough with it to have styles sometimes experience hypnogogic out of body experiences because they truly do have a certain amount of consciousness residing outside of their body. These sensations are sometimes the first hint that a person is capable of learning styles.

Executive Abstract

  • The emerging scientific theory of Orthodynamics proposes explanations for phenomena previously believed to be supernatural. A disinformation and monitoring campaign is currently in place to throttle the pace of this research and prevent leaks of classified information.
  • A consequence of the structure of space is the existence of the Yau, a shadow realm that mirrors normal space.
  • Phenomena such as the Cascade Effect and Styles are possible when a person is able to consciously control their connection to the Yau.
  • Some people are capable of vanishing from normal space by entering the Yau and passing through solid objects by going deeper into it.
  • Conscious entities exist in the Yau, most of which formed by being torn from a living or dead person. Interacting and communicating with these entities is only possible under extreme circumstances.
  • The universe is composed of an infinite number of alternate timelines, referred to as worldlines, each radiating out from the big bang. Inter-worldline interactions are possible under extreme circumstances and have occurred naturally in the passed.
  • Connections between worldlines can allow beings with truly alien phisiology and mindsets to access our world.

Orthodynamics

All descriptions of Yau and worldline cosmology discussed in this section are based on the current tenets of Orthodynamic Field Theory. Orthodynamics is an emerging theory that attempts to unify quantum mechanics and relativity by proposing the existence 9 orthogonal dimensions of space and a nested three-fold symmetry of all particles. The proposed dimensions include the three measurable dimensions we're familiar with (length, width, and height), the three tiny dimensions curled up at every point of conventional space that cause the Yau phenomenon (θ, ρ, and φ), and three time-like dimensions that result in the worldline phenomenon (line, plane, and radial). Unlike other attempts at unifying theories, orthodynamics makes predictions that closely correspond to classified observations as described in other security clearance keywords.

The field of orthodynamics promises immense societal benefits should it develop to the point of becoming an applied science, however, research into this field runs a high risk of compromising critical secrets classified under the COLD JULY security keyword. In an effort to prevent these leaks, all scholars and institutions engaged in orthodynamics research are closely monitored.

Early experiments in the field have thus far affirmed the theory's predictions, however, to prevent leaking classified information the peer review process of these experiments has been sabotaged and the results have been declared to be not reproducible. Additionally, a disinformation campaign has been instituted that derides the field of orthodynamics as untestable pseudoscience in order to keep growth in the field limited to controllable numbers.

The Yau

The Yau was named as an homage to the manifold structures in turn of the century string theory. At every point in physical space, there is a tiny toroidal-helix shaped manifold composed of the three dimensions θ, ρ, and φ. These manifolds are smaller than any of the particles that make up baryonic matter, and as such all matter floats on top of the manifold and cannot enter or directly interact with anything inside the Yau.

Some exotic particles, namely sterile leptons and achromatic quarks, are capable of of being stable at sizes small enough to fit within the Yau manifold. Particles this small naturally sink down into the Yau and then exist parallel to, but not influenced by, normal physical space. This phenomenon results in the manifestation of a second layer of space just below normal material space.

Anything in the Yau is typically causally isolated from physical space, however certain processes, such as flavor oscillation and spin conduction, can result in information being transferred between physical space and the Yau. Under extreme circumstances the Yau manifold can even be dilated enough for physical matter to enter the Yau. An object that has entered the Yau in this way is no longer visible, tangible or detectable by any means in normal space.

The fundamental forces operate differently in the Yau. Electromagnetism only operates at the distances needed to bind molecules and does not propagate any further. Chromodynamism performs it's normal function of binding atomic nuclei, but also extends a mild repulsive force from physical objects that fades out over several millimeters in the Yau. This repulsion is the cause of the soft and blurry, but still solid, boundaries physical objects project into the Yau. The effects of gravity also fade out over the course of several hundred meters, resulting noticeably weaker gravitation at differing altitudes.

Yau Depth

A being in the Yau can move freely throughout a realm that mirrors normal space. While it can't be detected from normal space, it experiences the shadows of all the matter in normal space. Physical objects appear as blurred silhouettes of their normal selves. The less something moves around in normal space the more solid it's Yau shadow is. For example, a building or landform would appear completely solid and opaque in the Yau while a moving car would be little more than a foggy wake of its motion.

An object's position on the Yau manifold's φ dimension describes how "deep" it is in the Yau. The deeper something gets, the less it is influenced by the shadows of matter in normal space. The depth an object's shadow extends into the Yau is determined by how often it moves. As an object remains still in reference to the local gravity well, it's repulsive residual chromodynamic force extends deeper and deeper into the Yau. In the very shallow Yau everything is completely solid. A little deeper in the Yau objects that are in constant motion are transparent enough that they can be passed through with ease, traffic and crowds no longer present any obstacles but walls and doors do. It is difficult to achieve depths greater than this, but at a medium depth doors and furniture give out, deeper and some newer buildings are gone too, deeper still and nothing is left but the earth. In the extremely deep Yau the landscape itself is nothing more than a blurry shade that can be easily moved through.

Yau Biology

Over the course of Earth's history, life gradually evolved the ability to utilize Yau scale interactions as part of the biological process. While it can't interact directly with the Yau, the nervous system of most vertebrates is nonetheless capable of indirectly influencing the spin of adjacent particles in the shallow Yau. This capability allows the organism to utilize the particles in its Yau shadow as a sort of external computation and sensory organ called the Yau body.

The Yau body originally evolved in fish as a mechanism to the senses by detecting nearby objects and motion. All higher order animals possess a Yau body, and in reptiles, birds, and mammals it evolved to supplement the brain and acts as long term memory storage. In fact, the physical brain has almost no capability for sustaining complex long term memories. In many higher order mammals the Yau body also acts as on-demand supplemental processing power in the brain, however this resource only seems to be accessed during the release of epinephrine.

In humans the Yau body is very highly developed and is also responsible for running a portion of the algorithm for consciousness. Conscious awareness arises from the constant process of comparing the brain's current state with a partial mirror of that state maintained by the Yau body. It's thought that this mirror originally evolved from the virtual state that gets created in dreaming animals as a mechanism for long term memory consolidation and error correction.

Entanglement

The bandwidth of one's brain to Yau body connection is referred to as Entanglement. Most people only possess just enough Entanglement to maintain consciousness. Severing this connection can result in dissociative episodes, loss of consciousness, or amnesia. Additionally, some people have a much greater degree of Entanglement, enough that they have a capability to intentionally control their Yau body that they may not be aware of.

People with awareness and control of their own Entanglement often find uses for it. Using any amount of it above what is needed to remain conscious occupies that portion of bandwidth for maintaining that connection and then recovering from its use over time. This effectively renders that portion unusable until it has recovered. Because of this, people experience Entanglement as a resource, a source of energy that can be used or depleted and regenerates over time.

The Cascade Effect

The primary goal of using Entanglement is to trigger the Cascade Effect.

When a particle exits or transmits information from the Yau, it has the side effect of dilating nearby Yau manifolds and making it much easier for its neighbors to do the same. This can result in a self-sustaining channel of communication between physical space and the Yau known as a cascade. These these two realms are thermodynamically separate, any communication from one of them is effectively the same as creating information in the other. This creation of information from nothing is highly anti-entropic, and, given a large enough cascade, can be strong enough to do raw thermodynamic work. This process of creating work from seemingly nothing is known as the Cascade Effect.

Styles

Those with extremely high degrees of Entanglement can learn to make use of the Cascade Effect. Any specific applied use of this effect is referred to as a Style.

In theory, the applied Cascade Effect could be used to bring any of the three fundamental forces (electromagnetism, chromodynamism, and gravitonertia) to bear on on any point in space that one's Yau body can reach. However, in practice the process of doing something that precise is too difficult to comprehend and control. Instead, styles manifest as Cascade Effects applied in primal ways to capabilities the body already has, namely improving physical and mental attributes. Only those extremely experienced with manipulating cascades and extending their Yau body can exert fine control over forces at a distance.

Cascade Events

Temporary spatially localized events have been observed to occur, naturally or otherwise, that can influence cascade processes. These events vary greatly in their size, intensity, and effects. These events can ease or hinder access to or from the Yau. They can even grant or sever significant amounts of Entanglement.

Spending any time inside any of these events can have unpredictable mental side effects on humans. The most common of these effects is creating dissociative experiences, while in more intense events memory loss or partial to total unconsciousness are common. Prolonged exposure to cascade events can even create permanent changes in one's brain or Yau body. Damage dealt by one in this way can manifest as personality disorders, or conversely, the Yau body can be made more robust, entangled, and aware from prolonged practice in resisting dissociation.

Sublimation

Styles are also capable of causing significant amounts of physical matter to sublime completely into the Yau. This capability is most commonly used to undetectably store objects outside of physical space. However, the true potential of sublimation is realized by shifting one's entire mind and body out of space for the purposes of travel, stealth, clandestine access, or Yau exploration.

While inside the Yau, any physical matter no longer interacts with itself and instead is effectively in suspended animation. A living body in the Yau does not require oxygen, water, or food, but it's brain is also suspended. Conscious awareness is only maintained by a self-aware Yau body, but without a mirror-state running in a physical brain consciousness slowly fades over extended periods of time. Upon returning to normal space, all physical processes resume as if uninterrupted.

Yau Body Entities

When a person's physical body dies, their Yau body dissipates shortly afterward. However, in rare circumstances, certain combinations of highly developed Yau bodies and high levels of dissociation can result in a severed Yau body becoming a separate entity with its own self-awareness and agency. This is most likely to happen in a death, near death, or coma situation that causes the body to shed the Yau body.

Once separated from its original mind a Yau body entity, or Yau spirit, can move freely about the Yau. The amount of awareness and agency that a severed Yau spirit has varies greatly. Some are just simple animalistic repeating loops of instinct lost in the seemingly alien Yauscape while others are practically complete copies of the original person's personality that retain varying amounts of their original memories.

Yau Body Dissociation

A free moving Yau spirit is most commonly created when its original body dies, however, most deaths do not result in a Yau spirit; they are only created when a person dies inside a highly dissociative environment, such during a cascade event. It's also possible, but rare, for powerful dissociative events such as a near death experience or an extremely massive sudden Entanglement gain or loss to cause a Yau body to separate, leaving the original body temporarily weakened but otherwise alive and ready to regenerate a new Yau body over time.

Experiencing a complete dissociation event of this magnitude typically has serious mental side effects, especially if the severed Yau body contained within it a substantial portion of its owner's personality. In general, the greater the amount of Entanglement a person has the more resistant they are to a dissociative event but the more mental side effects they suffer should this resistance fail.

A few hours after total dissociation event, a person's consciousness and mental faculties will have returned, however, there are still sometimes lingering side-effects. These effects most commonly include personality changes (these can be minor or extreme, and frequently involve developments resembling Axis II personality disorders), memory loss (anywhere from a short blackout to total retrograde amnesia), or in extreme cases temporarily diminished skills and mental attributes (the traits most often decreased are Intelligence and Wits). These mental effects are usually temporary, but may last weeks or months as the person acclimates to a new Yau body.

It is possible for a severed Yau spirit to re-associate with its original host body. If this happens any mental side effects the host body may have been suffering will be alleviated over the course of the next few hours. The likelihood of a reunion happening increases in proportion to the amount of awareness imparted into the Yau body. Assuming it wants to, a spirit with enough agency can simply follow its original body until the association becomes possible. This reunion typically becomes possible after the body has completely recovered from the dissociation event and is most easily accomplished during a period of diminished consciousness such as sleep.

Chromatoception

Beings in the Yau, including humans that are just visiting via sublimation, have very different senses than what we're familiar with. Sound does not exist at all, light doesn't propagate any meaningful distance, so vision is impossible. The only real sense beings in the Yau have is an omnidirectional object detection capability called chromatoception. This sense operates out to several meters by detecting the residual chromodynamic wakes that solid objects possess. These wakes are too diffuse and blurry for chromatoception to detect more than the rough shape of something, so texture, color, and complex shapes are not perceivable the Yau.

The lack of sound and light, and the weakness of chromatoception make communication between beings in the Yau very difficult. Speech and sign language are impossible. Text is indiscernible, unless each character is larger than about 30 centimeters and has three dimensional depth. A being can make short lived indentations by brushing against an physical object's Yau wake, so slow communication via large scale writing is possible between beings that share a written language. Marks made this way only last for several seconds. Styles and technologies that allow telepathy function normally in the Yau and even between the Yau and physical space.

Yau Entity Interactions

Yau entities are relatively common in the Yau but the vast majority of them are barely conscious and barely detectable. These tend to float aimlessly from place to place or follow repeating behavioral loops. Conscious spirits are rare and not always willing or able to communicate. The degree of awareness in a spirit slowly fades over the course of years until they are reduced to almost nothing.

A being in the Yau is incapable of interacting with an object that is in, or anchored to, physical space. This includes the Yau bodies of living things and any objects that have been placed into the Yau by styles or technology. People that have used the style Sublimation to bring their entire body into the Yau can, however, interact with each other and any objects they have on their person as long as they stay at similar depths.

Worldline Cosmology

The universe we experience is a bigger place than we can imagine, but it is only a small part of a whole. Every moment of our entire universe is arranged in order as a line referred to as a worldline. At one end of our worldline is the beginning of time and at the other, the present moment. As we travel down our worldline we can only experience the present moment. A seemingly infinite number of worldlines radiate outward from the big bang. Every minuscule decision forges a new branch on its worldline, meaning almost any alternate history you can imagine has happened somewhere.

Most worldlines propagate as straight orderly branches, resulting in an overall multiverse with a structure roughly resembling a fractal snowflake. Smaller portions of the multiverse are less stable with worldlines that twist, turn, intertwine, jockey for space, and choke each other off. The angle, shape, and position of a worldline influences the values of the physical constants in the universe within it. The more distant two worldlines are, the more these physical constants governing things like the nature of matter or the speed of light may vary. In fact, relatively few of worldlines are even capable of sustaining solid matter, let alone stars, planets, and intelligent life.

Worldlines that are closer together have much more in common. Two adjacent ones may be indistinguishable except for some minor quirk of history. However, some twists and turns have brought radically dissimilar worldlines together and some worldlines have even become so intertwined that the boundaries between them have become less clear.

Every worldline is composed of two symmetrical parts; the three-dimensional physical space we are familiar with and its parallel Yau space. The physical space portion of each worldline is wholly separate from all others, its contents cannot interact with the contents of other lines. However, the Yau space is less clearly defined and in tightly packed parts of the multiverse it will sometimes blend together with the Yau space of adjacent lines. In such spaces anything capable of entering and traversing the deep Yau could potentially gain access to the adjacent worldlines.

Worldline Incursions

Worldline incursion events can occur when two lines become pushed together tightly enough that the worldline's Yau spaces become enmeshed. When this happens it becomes possible for an object in the Yau of one worldline to enter the Yau of the other one. If this object is capable of exiting the Yau, it can then do so in the other world. During an incursion only the deepest part of the Yau becomes enmeshed with the neighboring worldline and the incursion can only be detected or crossed by the most determined of Yau travelers.

Incursions can be isolated single events or ongoing cycles that cause repeating incursions every time a certain number of years passes. Incursions can also vary in intensity; greater intensity events become more detectable because they allow things to cross from one worldline to the other at increasingly shallow depths in the Yau.

Worldline incursions actually happen quite frequently, but most of them go unnoticed because they are just incursions with nearby worldlines that are so similar that they are practically identical. However, it is possible for more distant, and therefore more different, worldlines to be brought together. Incursions can last any amount of time from minutes to centuries.

Most incursions result in contact with an alternate worldline's version of Earth. While most of the time the two Earths are almost identical it is possible to connect two Earths at different points in history, two earths with different histories, or even two Earths with different evolutionary history populated by entirely different life.

While possible, worldline incursions that result in the contact with more distant worldlines are exceedingly rare. Many variables need to be just right in order for this to happen. A distant worldline may have different physics that don't allow for stars, planets, and life. While physically similar worldlines are attracted to one another, the odds of connecting with one that has a habitable planet near your location are small.

Incursion Entities

A sentient being that enters our worldine from another is known as an incursion entity. The vast majority of these entities are Yau spirits that do not possess any physical form. The deepest parts of the Yau are populated with lost and confused entities from wide variety of truly alien worldlines. Circumstances capable of transferring entities with physical bodies between worldlines are very rare, but have been documented in the past.

Many incursion entities from nearby worldlines are phisiologically identical to normal humans, but come from worldlines that are at a different point in history or that have an entirely different history. These entities are referred to as chronological isolates. A human entity brought to a different time is often confused and lacks any way to return to their own worldline.

In the 1960s an incursion that lasted most of the decade occured and resulted in hundreds of entities entering our world. The chraracteristics of these beings are discussed in documents classified as CIRCUMSTANCE MONO.

During the Ordovician period an incursion estimated to have lasted at least several centuries occured. All evidence for the existence of the entities that accessed our world during this period is discussed in documents classified as VOSTOK BEACHFRONT.

Entities from very distant worldlines may be profoundly alien to us. The possibilities of extremely alien, hostile, and powerful entities gaining access to our worldline is discussed in documents classified as COLD JULY.

The Liaison Program

Multiple three-letter agencies in the US have small divisions tasked with investigating, containing, and covering up supernatural events, but the Liaison Program is usually the first to arrive on the scene.

The program is technically part of the US Department of Defense, and ostensibly its mission is to acquire weaponizable incursion artifacts for the US military. However, in practice they are given an lot of operational leeway and will often opt to secretly keep artifacts internal to the organization.

Artifacts are chosen to be kept internal if their non-military applications are promising, if they are needed as equipment for Liaison agents, or if they would be too terrifying in the hands of the military.

Континентальный Власть
(The Continental Authority)

A Sino-Soviet agency dedicated to covering up supernatural events. A quirk of history and international treaty brought the Chinese and Russian halves of this agency together, but they often do not cooperate well.

Competition and secrecy between the agency's two halves often results in agents from one half arriving on a scene before anyone else, hiding any evidence and then leaving with any artifacts before anyone else can respond.

The Elusain

The Elusain are a small population of beings that ended up on Earth as the result of a massive worldline incursion. This incursion happened repeatedly with varying levels of intensity over the course of the 1900s and reached its peak in the late 60's. This incursion is also the source of a substantial portion of artifact technology, most of which appears to be Elusain in origin.

The Elusain are a humanoid species with an evolutionary heritage primarily consisting of a group of semi-aquatic, semi-arboreal creatures well adapted to life in the oxygen-poor but nutrient rich forested swampland common in their native worldline's version of Earth. Elusain have smooth hairless skin that varies in color from light tans and yellows to dark greenish browns. Some have darker dorsal and lighter ventral coloration patterns as a vestige of their aquatic heritage. They have long slim bodies supported a flexible lordosis curved spine and are typically 6 to 7 feet in height. Their long streamlined limbs terminate in highly dextrous hands and feet well adapted for grasping, climbing, and swimming with four long slender semi-webbed digits opposed by two thumb-like digits. The Elusain have rather minimal facial features, dominated by a pair of large, dark, lidless eyes protected with a nictating membrane. They have no mouth or nose, but rather a large triangular nasal slit protected and partially hidden by folds of skin.

Elusain Physiology

The Elusain are well adapted for, but also heavily dependent on, water. While they do not possess gills and must breath air they can stay comfortably submerged in oxygenated water for up to three hours by absorbing small amounts of oxygen transdermally. Elusain must completely submerge themselves frequently.

Elusain Artifacts

At some point in the past century a massive worldline incursion resulted in a large number of artifacts from a distant worldline being deposited around the globe. While these rare artifacts include everything from cultural objects and personal effects to advanced technology, but the most commonly represented items are small pieces of advanced military hardware including small arms, personal armor, and computer components. Characters can acquire these arms and equipment to augment their capabilities, but perhaps the largest impact on our world is caused by the computing hardware.
